Taak Lake was a small, fresh-water lake in Alpuk north of the Cold Lands .

Geography

Taak Lake was located off of the Utaak Stream , at the base of the Lugsaas Chain near the gap in those mountains that led to the village of Ishe in Vaasa . It was southwest of the Iulutiun village of Puttak .

History

Prior to the 15 th century DR , Taak Lake was on the Great Glacier . It was frozen most of the year since it was only 10 feet (3 meters) at its deepest point and because of the bitter cold of the region. By the late 15 th century DR, the Glacier had continued its centuries-long retreat further north, freeing the lake. The settlement of Frohm's Rest had been established on the lake's northern shore.
