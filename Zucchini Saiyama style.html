Zucchini Saiyama style was the name given to a dish of the Ancients from the Living Jungle of the Malatran Plateau .

Description

Zucchini Saiyama style was a stewed vegetable dish made using all the ingredients native to the Malatran Plateau. The recipe called for sweating diced onions , red bell peppers , with garlic . Once the vegetables turned tender, diced tomatoes and zucchini were added and simmered together. The stew was seasoned with salt , black pepper, and oregano . Lastly, cooked cannelloni beans were added. Once all the ingredients were done, the stew was served on ancient ceramic platters on a bed of cooked rice .

History

In the Year of Despair of the Malatran calendar ( 1375 DR ), a band of heroes of the Living Jungle were recruited by Reesta , the chief of the Saiyama tribe, to recover an ancient plate of ceramic from a ruin of the Ancients. The adventurers embarked on their quest, thinking they were recovering a great treasure. Instead, they realized that the plate held a recipe for a dish of the Ancients, later named zucchini Saiyama style. Later, Reesta and his adventurers gathered the ingredients and participated in a great cooking competition among the jungle tribes. The Nubari were surprised to find out that the jungle's spear-shaped zucchini were not just weed and garuda food, but also a delicious edible vegetable.
