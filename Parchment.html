Parchment was a high-quality writing surface made from animal hide, capable of holding ink , paint , and other pigments.

Description

Parchment was similar to other writing materials like vellum , paper , and papyrus , and was relatively commonplace. It was made of animal hide, often goat or sheepskin .

Availability

Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue offered high-quality parchment sheets for 1 gp each, alongside various other writing surfaces and tools. It also sold packs of 50 sheets of gridded parchment for 45 gp, for use in mapmaking.

In Waterdeep , the League of Skinners and Tanners handled all aspects of tanning and leatherworking , including the production of parchment.

Calaunt was a great producer and exporter of leatherwork including parchment.

Usage

Projects such as building plans, maps, academic treatises, and wizard 's tomes used parchment scrolls and books.

Parchment was a fine material for creating spell scrolls , producing more reliable results than papyrus , though not as well as vellum.
