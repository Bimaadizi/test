Lord Archibald Ravenshade was a patriar member of the Ravenshade family in Baldur's Gate sometime in or before the late 15 th century DR .

Description

Archibald was a haughty-looking half-elven man with greenish-tinted skin, long wavy brown hair, and well-groomed facial hair.

Activities

Lord Ravenshade was known as a master author and playwright who penned sonnets and plays about "ambition, supernatural tensions with the natural world, avarice, love, and moving bushes." One of his plays was called Porklet .

History

In the Year of Three Ships Sailing, 1492 DR , a portrait of Archibald Ravenshade painted by Oskar Fevras was kept in the attic of the Jannath estate of Baldur's Gate. That year, it became possessed by wailing poltergeist unwittingly summoned by the painter. The picture whispered alluringly, only to shriek later, startling observers. Other copies of the same painting were seen across the city of Baldur's Gate.
