The Funada clan were the ruling family in Nakamaru in Wa around 1357 DR . The Funada held the position of daimyo and of many key city officers, plus some members held important positions in local temples. They kept a close eye over their vassals thanks to their allegiance with the ninja of Hijo clan and to many lesser samurai families, who wanted to improve their own positions.
