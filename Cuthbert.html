Earl Cuthbert was a ruler of Cuthbert Fief in Hartsvale in the late 14 th century DR .

Description

Cuthbert was a barrel-chested, round-faced man. He was balding and had squinting eyes. He dressed in a jerkin with the symbol of his fiefdom, crossed shepherd's staves, displayed.

Relationships

Earl Cuthbert was married and had at least two daughters.

Possessions

Cuthbert was in possession of a library within his castle that contained many biotite folios written in Metamorpherie , taken from the stone giants his ancestors had conquered. He claimed to have read all of them carefully.

When in combat, Cuthbert wielded a battleaxe and wore steel armor.

Activities

Earl Cuthbert always sent his taxes to Castle Hartwick on time, a matter that did not go unnoticed by the queen.

History

In 1366 DR , when Princess Brianna returned from her abduction by Goboka , Cuthbert was one of the first of the earls to support Brianna in deposing her father King Camden .

During the battle in Cuthbert Castle against the false Prince Arlien , Earl Cuthbert tried to buy the queen, Tavis , and Avner time by blocking the doorway to the folio room. His battleaxe was unable to break through Arlien's magic armor, and the ettin slew him with a single blow that cleaved both his armor and his sternum.
