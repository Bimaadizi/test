Giant sea anemones were a monstrous species of the creatures of the same name , which many mistook for plants.

Description

These creatures had a stout central body that was 8 feet (2.4 meters) in diameter and crowned with a gaping maw that was 6 feet (1.8 meters) in diameter. Surrounding their mouths were a number of tentacles, with the average amount being around a hundred. Each tentacle was covered in hundreds of small, barbed, hook-like needles that would spring forth once a prey was in their grasp.

Anemones had very colorful bodies that displayed colors of blue, green, pink, red, orange, purple, or some combination of them.

Behavior

These creatures possessed no form of higher intelligence, having intellects below that of most animals.

Abilities

The barbed hooks that covered the tentacles of anemones would inject prey with a paralytic poison , which could be negated with the spell neutralize poison . So long as a tentacle was attached to a creature, the poison would continue to advance.

Any tentacle that was severed from an anemone's body would regrow at a rate of 1 foot (0.3 meters) per day. The base of the creature however, could not regenerate.

Combat

The tentacles of anemones would very swiftly ensnared any creatures that came within 30 feet (9.1 meters) of them and draw the prey towards their maw. Anemones typically would attempt to attach at least three tentacles to their prey. If a prey was inedible or too large, it would release them once they became paralyzed. If an anemone was full, then it would simply hold on to its prey for later.

When seriously threatened a giant sea anemone would draw their tentacles all the way back into their central body.

Species

Ecology

These were often encountered in a stationary position, but were capable of moving themselves at a very slow rate. Most rarely chose to do so, remaining in one spot for the duration of their lives.

These creatures reproduced by budding, with young growing out from their base. Once sufficiently grown it would break off and move to its own feeding ground.

Diet

Giant sea anemones were a carnivorous species.

Habitats

At depths greater than 50 ft (15 m), giant sea anemones were typically found solitarily. In tidal pools and secluded shorelines, smaller varieties of them could be found in groups numbering up to fifty or even two hundred. These smaller varieties would fasten themselves to rocks.

These creatures could be found in the Sea of Fallen Stars .

Beyond Toril , they could be found in the oceans of the planet Krynn .

Relationships

Some creatures were unaffected by the paralytic poison of giant sea anemones, either from being immune to it or from having thick hides. This included certain brightly colored fish, known as clownfish , which would hide inside them from predators and feed on the scraps they left behind. Manta rays , who could often be found swimming among them. And barracudas , who preyed upon their tentacles.

Some aquatic races kept giant sea anemones as guard animals, fighting them just enough to keep them from wandering away.
