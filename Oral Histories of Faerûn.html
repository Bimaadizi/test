Oral Histories of Faerûn was a lore book that collected stories and sagely musings on a variety of different topics, published before 1492 DR .

Description

This tome was a thin book with a gold -trimmed cover. Numerous scraps of paper with previous owners' notes and annotations could be found inside.

Content

Chapter 4: Gith and Mind Flayers

Oral Histories of Faerûn: Gith and Mind Flayers was authored by a writer who spoke with Pallidor the Swift , a 700-year-old wood elf from the Wood of Sharp Teeth . The chapter told about the author earning the old elf's trust in order to record his tales. The following poem was told by a warm campfire and Pallidor swore the story was true.

Chapter 7: Paladin Oathbreakers

Oral Histories of Faerûn: Paladin Oathbreakers was written by a writer who spoke with a human paladin oathbreaker , Valemor the Outcast .

Chapter 25: The Spellplague

Oral Histories of Faerûn: The Spellplague was written by a dragonborn author who spoke to two halfling brothers from Luiren , Cornelius and Tomelius Worthywart . They spoke of the loss of their homeland during the catastrophic Spellplague , debating whether it was consumed in blue fire or sunk beneath the waters of the Great Sea .

History

In 1492 DR, copies of this book could be found throughout the Western Heartlands .
