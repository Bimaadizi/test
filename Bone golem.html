Bone golems , sometimes referred to as skeletal golems , were terrifying grisly constructs assembled from bones of various creatures and, despite their appearance, were not undead creatures.

Description

Bone golems usually 6 feet (1.8 meters) tall creatures, created by collecting and affixing together bones in a roughly humanoid form. Some golems were constructed with four arms, allowing them to wield four one-handed or two two-handed weapons, while others were given deadly claws, or even bone scythes. Oftentimes, bone golems were put together using bones from various creators, from beasts to humanoids. This gave them a terrifying appearance of something spawned by nightmares.

Abilities

These fearsome constructs could unleash a blood-curdling laugh that filled the hearts of their opponents with terror. This ability could cause the victims to become paralyzed with fear or even die of extreme fright.

These constructs were invulnerable to non-magical weapons and were unaffected by sleep , charm , hold , and similar spells . Like other constructs, they were safe from gases, as well as immune to fire, cold, and electricity. Bone golems were not undead and could not be turned. However, bone golems were susceptible to the shatter spell as it caused them to become vulnerable to any physical damage. Despite their appearance, bone golems were not undead and shared no vulnerabilities of such creatures imbued with the magic of the Negative Energy plane .

Combat

Like many other constructs, bone golems were not intelligent creatures and could only follow the most basic commands. They could not devise plans of attack nor use clever strategies. They took on a direct approach in battle.

Creation

Bone golem creation required collecting and affixing vast amounts of bones together, and then the casting of animate dead , symbol of fear , binding , and wish spells onto the bones. Often, it would be a mixture of animal, monster, and humanoid bones in the same golem, giving the creature a nightmarish appearance. It often took as much as 2 months of work and could cost as much as 35,000 gold pieces .
