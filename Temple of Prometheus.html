The Temple of Prometheus was a temple dedicated to the Olympian god Prometheus in the city of Medinat Muskawoon .

Location

The temple was due east of the Great Hall of the Pasha in Medinat Muskawoon. It faced west and slightly south, with its back towards the rising sun in remembrance of the griffon-vulture that would devour Prometheus's liver each morning.

Structure

The temple was just twenty columns on a raised platform, holding up the roof 30 feet (9.1 meters) above the platform. There was only one wall, facing east, with a 12‑inch (0.3‑meter) diameter window, and a brazier before it.

History

There were several temples to Prometheus in the fertile valley of the river Athis , brought by the travelers from other realms far removed from Realmspace .

Rumors & Legends

Torches lit from this shrine had the power to drive away the conjured asps in the Temple of Ra . If lit by the sun's rays concentrated by the round window behind it, these torches would not stop burning for as long as they remained in Medinat Muskawoon.
