The Maiden Danced at Dawn was a small inn located in the hamlet of Dawngleam in Cormyr around 1368 DR .

Description

The rooms of the inn were somewhat cramped. Humidity was an issue, and the inn always felt damp.

Activities

The inn was renowned, at least locally, for a brandy made in-house called Maiden's Kiss . A flask of Maiden's Kiss cost 5 silver pieces .

Simple fare consisting of sausage , bread , biscuits , and nut cakes could be had at the rate of 4 copper pieces per plate. A selection of ales and stouts were available, as well as a decent assortment of wines .
