Brachiosauruses ( pronounced : / b r ɑː tʃ i oʊ ˈ s ɑːr ʌ s / bratch-ee-o-SAR-us ) were a type of large and incredibly heavy dinosaur, similar to a brontosaurus .

Description

Brachiosaurs were considered to be among some of the heaviest dinosaurs to ever live, weighing around 85​ to ​90 tons (77,000​ to ​82,000 kilograms). Their bodies were around 60 feet (18 meters) in length.

Reputation

Though peaceful, due to their immense size brachiosaurs had a tendency to accidentally step on smaller creatures and thus could pose a threat to adventurers .

Ecology

Diet

Brachiosaurs were a herbivorous species of dinosaur.

Habitats

These creatures were known to inhabit lakes and swamps in regions with a warm climate.
