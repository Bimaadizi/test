Manshoon ( pronounced : / m ɑː n ˈ ʃ uː n / man-SHOON ) was the founder of the Zhentarim , also known as the Black Network, until he was "slain" by Fzoul Chembryl , his ally, and Lord Orgauth in 1370 DR . However, it transpired that Manshoon had created a dozen or more clones that the murderous duo then found themselves facing. Only three of these clones were known to have survived into the late 1370s DR and once more headed the evil organization known as the Zhentarim.

Personality

Completely fitting the archetype of the sly-tongued, sinister, and decadent master spellcaster, Manshoon ruthlessly crushed revolts within his own ranks of the Black Network, easily slaughtered his rivals and weeded the weak out the ranks of the young and ambitious by sending upstarts on nigh-impossible suicide missions. Hated and mistrusted by Fzoul, Manshoon manipulated him much as he did all others—remaining arrogant, untouchable in his power, and faintly amused by it all.

Relations

He had contacts with many creatures, mainly beholders , such as Xarlandralath and Xualahuu , some of whom held much respect and loyalty to him. For example, Xualahuu was deeply loyal to him and tried to advance the Black Network's plots.

History

Early Life

Manshoon was born in Zhentil Keep in the Year of the Carrion Crow, 1229 DR , to Harlshoon , the first lord of Zhentil Keep. Manshoon, his brother Asmuth ; and Chess , the son of their father's chief rival, Calkontor , were sent off together as adventurers to prove themselves worthy of being lords like their fathers. While his sons were away, Harlshoon was poisoned by Calkontor, but, with his dying breaths, he threw Calkontor out of a tower window. The trio's fathers' places were usurped by a pair of opportunists, Theilon Greencloak and Ulsan Baneservant , the deaths blamed on foreign assassins, while the unknowing lord-princes continued their adventures together.

One such adventure was a quest to take a magical sword from a barrow, a goal shared by a trio of female Harpers . Asmuth was blinded and Manshoon had one of his hands blown off when he carelessly grabbed for the sword. Chess saved both of their lives, and the three fled when the sword animated itself, appearing to attack the Harpers. They came across a party of other adventurers and murdered them all in their sleep. Manshoon recovered a wand and a spellbook . He traded the wand for his hand to be restored but kept the spellbook as he secretly planned to study the Art , having been inspired by the magic of the sword. After a few more adventures they headed home, learning about their slain sires and their usurped positions in an inn somewhere in the Dalelands . Manshoon made sure his blind brother Asmuth fell off a bridge to his death before they returned to the keep where he and Chess planned to dispose of the usurpers.

Meanwhile, Theilon Greencloak had gone to fight some mages in Thar and died in the conflict while Ulsan Baneservant remained in the keep. Manshoon and Chess were attacked on arrival by assassins acting on Ulsan's orders but were saved by Manshoon's magic. However, unknown to him, Chess defected in exchange for his father's seat on the council. It was, however a double cross: Chess, Manshoon, and their childhood friend Fzoul Chembryl killed the usurper. They claimed their rightful council seats and supported Fzoul's rise through the ranks of the Banite priesthood.

Building the Zhentarim

Feeling exposed, Manshoon immediately set about creating a cabal of mages, his Black Cloaks , to protect him, recruiting Fzoul early on. Not long afterward, in the Year of Bright Dreams, 1261 DR , he founded the Zhentarim. However, knowing the priests would obey Bane over anyone else and assuming the wizards were just as ambitious as he was, Manshoon held little trust for any of his new allies. He sent out spies and was determined to raise his powers in the arcane far above anyone else in Zhentil Keep. Some of his spies returned from Teshendale talking of a flying hollow rock. Manshoon investigated personally, hoping to find powerful magic. Instead, he discovered the beholder Xantriph . The two canny individuals joined forces, claiming the rock was a conduit to the gods. Banite priests flocked to the place where Xantriph instructed them all to do as Manshoon commanded, joining the Zhentarim and leading them away from the decadent High Imperceptor of the church. However, in order to do this, Manshoon and his magelings had to pledge fealty to Bane, promising to lead the faith to even greater heights of power than the weakening High Imperceptor had. Furthermore, Manshoon promised control of the priesthood and Zhentil Keep to the beholders, or so it seemed.

In secret, Manshoon embarked on another plan for his organization. Having little interest in empire-building, he set his Zhentarim wizards the task of establishing trade routes between the Moonsea and the untapped markets of the Sword Coast . This allowed Manshoon to further dream of advancing his skill in the Art while manipulating both the beholders and the church of Bane from behind the scenes. During this period, Manshoon developed his ultimate insurance plan, the notorious stasis clone spell.

The Clones and the Manshoon Wars

In the Year of the Tankard, 1370 DR , Manshoon was "slain" by Fzoul and Lord Orgauth, who then found themselves facing around a dozen clones that the wizard had created. Each of the clones was convinced that he was the real Manshoon and was inexplicably compelled to slay his rival clones. Most of these perished in the ensuing battles. The caches of magical items formerly hidden by Manshoon were scattered throughout the Realms. These conflicts were known as the Manshoon Wars .

Three clones, who mastered this killing compulsion by means of spells and devices, survived, with the one that returned to the Zhentarim appearing to be 'the' Manshoon.

One of the three remaining clones returned to the Zhentarim, conceding leadership to Fzoul and entering into some sort of accommodation with him. He served as a member-at-large, a free agent with the power and authority to direct Zhentarim assets as he saw fit. Used to ruling and manipulating from behind the scenes and relieved of responsibility of administering to the Black Network, the Zhentarim Manshoon seemed to have learned a lesson in caution and patience. By 1372 DR , he had redoubled his arcane research, seeking ways to transfer his consciousness from one clone to another at will.

The second clone assumed a position of power among the Night Masks of Westgate , calling himself Orbakh or Orlak II . This Manshoon awoke at the beginning of the Manshoon Wars only to find that he had been turned into a vampire and was not in the stasis chamber with the rest of the Manshoon clones (or the stash of magic items he had left for himself in the event of his death). He hunted down the vampire who had turned him, the Night King Orlak , and slew him, taking the vampire lord's magic items and his title of Night King. Whilst venturing up to the surface to feed, he found himself in Westgate where the magic items influenced him to create some vampire underlings and, with them, he decided to take over and revitalize the Night Masks thieves' guild. Orbakh quickly took over the Night Masks as he arrived in the midst of a power vacuum left by the death of the guild's former leader. He restructured the guild to his tastes and planned to turn Westgate into the capital city of a vampire kingdom by wedding the last in the line of the defunct Westgate royalty. This plot never saw fruition, however, because of the intervention of the Spellplague . [ speculation ]

The third took refuge in Undermountain with Halaster .

Although most believed the Manshoon Wars persisted only a few short, chaotic years, they were actually waged over the course of at least a century. The surviving clones became increasingly entrenched, hoping to outlast their foes.

Post-Spellplague

When Returned Netheril learned of the Zhentarim's alliance with the phaerimm , the shadovar destroyed Zhentil Keep, killing the Zhentarim Manshoon. The one in Undermountain appeared to have died during the events of the Spellplague itself. Now only the vampire Manshoon known as Orbakh remained. He was now the Night King , leader of the Night Masks and a powerful vampire. The Fire Knives though, purged Westgate of vampires.

Retaking his original name, the last Manshoon clone relocated to the haunted castle of Stormwatch , and rebuilt the forces of the Zhentarim, adding his own undead thralls to those few living Zhents that remained. Stormwatch and Darkhold were the main bases of these forces, but not having the resources to rebuild the organization to its former glory, the Manshoon's new Zhentarim was now merely a mercenary company, bolstered by priests of Cyric from Darkhold.

Decades after the collapse of the Weave and the devastating effects of the Spellplague , the vampiric Manshoon began plotting to take rulership of Cormyr . To this end, he slowly began collecting information on items rumored to contain the spirits of the Nine . Known as blueflame ghosts , combining their power was rumored to be strong enough to breach the wards protecting the royal palace of Suzail . He also began to use his powerful mental techniques on the lowly and powerful alike, enslaving some, making others mind-thralls to be used or discarded as needed (without the person being any the wiser), and spying on the nobility and palace staff.

Manshoon also began to slowly whittle away at Elminster's defenses, killing an ally of the Old Sage's or removing one of the many contingency spells still tied to him whenever he felt like having some fun. Finally, in the Year of the Ageless One, 1479 DR , he took revenge upon Elminster for the foiling of so many plans and the destruction of so many clones. Manshoon, using the body of a beholder held in mind-thrall, unleashed magics strong enough to reduce Elminster to dust. He departed, however, before discovering that Elminster survived as a pile of magical ash.

Manshoon continued to plot his overthrow of the Obarskyrs . He followed Elminster to the Simbul 's cave in Shadowdale with the intent of killing Elminster. However, Elminster and Storm Silverhand were able to restore the Simbul's sanity, and she forced Manshoon to yield and promise to work with Elminster to recover blueflame items . After the Simbul teleported away, Manshoon once again blasted Elminster's human form to ashes but couldn't destroy him.

Later, after Mystra's return around 1480 DR , Elminster regained his original body and destroyed the vampire Manshoon for good. However, Elminster knew that Manshoon was ready to awaken a new clone.

Post-Second Sundering

Although intending to continue where his former clone left off, the new Manshoon, a vampire again, continuously found himself thwarted, from even attempting to begin his schemes, by the newly returned Mirt the Moneylender , though the two of them regularly met for drinks and passive-aggressive conversations in Suzail , Cormyr. When the events in Myth Drannor led to a disturbance in the Weave in the Year of the Rune Lords Triumphant, 1487 DR , the vampire's guard dropped momentarily. With Mirt's help, he was arrested by War Wizards in Memories of Queen Fee .

In the Year of Dwarvenkind Reborn, 1488 DR , Manshoon, now no longer interested in direct confrontations with the Sage of Shadowdale , was caught alongside several other archwizards inside a spellstorm in Cormyr for the purpose of making a bid for the legendary Lost Spell . However, aside from an "alliance" made with Malchor Harpell (at the indirect request of Elminster and Mystra ), Manshoon eventually left empty-handed.

By 1492 DR , the Undermountain Manshoon had resurfaced in Waterdeep, seemingly having survived the Spellplague and his clash with Halaster Blackcloak. The ancient archwizard had infiltrated Waterdeep years before and had been hiding out in Kolat Towers ever since, plotting to replace the City Watch with Black Network mercenaries that were loyal to him alone and to rule Waterdeep as Wizard-King.
