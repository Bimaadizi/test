Daggerford clarry was a blended wine made out of various grapes that were grown by the farmers outside of Daggerford , Liam's Hold , and Gillian's Hill .

History

In the Year of Wild Magic, 1372 DR , Daggerford clarry was one of the most popular local wines produced in Daggerford. The clarry was a more expensive option and a staple at the Black Stone Inn . In the Year of Three Ships Sailing, 1492 DR , A Modest Guide to Sword Coast Wine stated that Daggerford clarry was as suitable for consumption as " bugbear taint."
