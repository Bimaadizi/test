The Statue of Kochi was a family heirloom that belonged to Okuzo of Westgate , a Wanese immigrant who ran Okuzo's Imports in the late 14 th century DR .

Description

The Statue of Kochi was a depiction of Emperor Kochi , the first Emperor of Wa . History

The Statue of Kochi belonged to Okuzo's family for numerous generations and served as a symbol of the family's heritage. When she left home for the western city of Westgate, Okuzo's father insisted she take the heirloom with her to remind the young woman of home. Okuzo's father seemed not to understand his daughter and hoped that the statue would bring Okuzo home sooner rather than later.
