The Boneyard was a shop in the Upper Trade Lanes of Skullport in the late 14 th century DR .

Structure

The Boneyard was a one-story building in fair-to-poor condition. A bulette skull was prominently displayed above the shop's entrance.

Interior

The interior of the shop consisted of crude wooden shelves stacked floor to ceiling with skeletal remains of varying sizes, ages, and species.

Services

The Boneyard specialized in the trade of bones . Single bones from common demihuman species could be acquired for as little as 5 silver pieces , while a complete and well-preserved skeleton began at 30 gold pieces .

Inhabitants

The shop was owned by Dethyn Hurl .
