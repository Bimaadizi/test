Salabar Street was a hard-packed dirt and gravel road in the Trades Ward of Waterdeep . The southernmost part of this road was sometimes referred to as Moorewalk .

Geography

This street ran due southwest off of the Court of the White Bull , intersected River Street , and then curved southeast until terminating on Buckle Street . At the midpoint of Salabar Street, before it intersected with River Street, it acted as the western terminus of Sorn Street and the eastern terminus of River Square .
