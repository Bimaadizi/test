The temple of the God-Kings in Bezantur was the only temple to the Mulhorandi pantheon that was allowed to exist in Thay as of the mid-to-late 14 th century DR .

Location

This temple was located outside the walls of Bezantur, the so-called "City of a Thousand Temples".

Structure

The temple was a ziggurat, the traditional symbol of the Mulhorandi pantheon.

Activities

While the worship of the God-Kings of Mulhorand was repressed by the Red Wizards of Thay , many commonfolk continued to honor them as the gods of their ancestors. Owing to the hostility toward the religion, the high priest of this temple was frequently jailed on accusations of sedition.

History

In the Year of the Prince, 1357 DR , the high priest was jailed for more than three months, leaving a junior priest to tend to the temple.
