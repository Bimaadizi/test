The Tannath Mountains were the southern part of the Dragonjaw Mountains , starting south of the River Umber . They were recognized as a territory belonging to Aglarond , and formed its border with Thay to the east.

Geography

The craggy range was made of rugged, tall mountains of fault block origin. Wide passes flanked them on both sides, the Tannath Gap on the west and the Shyvar Pass on the east.

Flora & Fauna

Griffons were known to dwell in the range. There were also birds and mountain goats . The slopes of the range were very bare.

Government

The Tannath Mountains were considered part of Aglarond.

Trade

Silver was mined out of the mountains at some point before 1479 DR .

Defenses

The region served as a buffer between Aglarond and Thay, making for an obstacle which Thayan armies found almost impassable for any troop that did not fly. Aglarondan patrols in the region were minimal, originating in the Glarondar fortress. Most Thayan forces employed different routes.

History

During Brindor 's reign, in 1369 , he built the citadel Glarondar and the Watchwall with the aid of the local galeb duhr in 1085 DR .

In 1479 DR , Captain Arol of the Watchwall forces tried to cut a deal with local goblins in order to get them to provide advance warning of any threats heading their way.

Rumors & Legends

There were rumors in 1479 DR that goblins had settled in the silver mines.

Notable Locations

The Watchwall led from the mountains towards Glarondar in the Yuirwood . Some silver mines had been abandoned by 1479 DR .

Inhabitants

The zone was not inhabited by humanoids in 1372 DR , the peaks having never been settled; by 1479 DR , at least one tribe of goblins had settled in the region, and stone giants were known to dwell there. Galeb duhr populated the mountains since before 1085 DR . An ancient fang dragon , Nartheling , dwelt at Umbergoth as of 1373 DR .
