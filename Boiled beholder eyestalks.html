Boiled beholder eyestalks was a common and gruesome dish of githyanki cuisine found in gith crèches and the Astral Sea in the late 15 th century DR .

Description

This straightforward dish was made using beholder eyestalks, severed at the base and boiled whole, skin and eyeballs included. They were served whole, plated on large trays, and garnished with sprigs of parsley and lemon wedges, presumably for drizzling over this gourmet meat.
