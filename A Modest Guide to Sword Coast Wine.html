A Modest Guide to Sword Coast Wine was a sommelier book with a self-explanatory title, published sometime in or before the late 15 th century DR .

Content

As it was evident from the title, the guide touched upon a variety of wines and sherries produces across the Sword Coast region of Faerûn . The book praised such vintages as Berduskan Dark and Arabellan Dry . It complimented Elverquisst 's "sinuous fruity flavours," and favorably mentioned throat-slamming notes of Red Dragon Crush , notably not produced on the Sword coast but found there via adventurers and traders who dealt with the Vistani folk of the Domains of Dread . Thayan wines were praised for their red color and Saerloonian Glowfire for its pale hues. Lastly, the guide's author proclaimed that mulled or fortified wines, such as zzar and Daggerford clarry to be as suitable for drinking as bugbear 's nether region.

History

In the Year of Three Ships Sailing, 1492 DR , a copy of A Modest Guide to Sword Coast Wine could be found in the kitchen of The Singing Lute in Baldur's Gate 's Lower City .
