The tui-fa , also known as the tonfa ( pronounced : / t oʊ n f ɑː / tohn-fah ), was a weapon unique to the continent of Kara-Tur .

Description

A tui-fa was a hardwood rod with a small off-center handle protruding from its side. Spanning a length of approximately 2​ to ​3 feet (61​ to ​91 centimeters), it was originally a millstone handle but has since evolved into a martial arts implement.

With the tui-fa, a skilled practitioner could proficiently engage in blocking, weapon interception, weapon destruction, and effective offensive maneuvers. It was often used in regions where conventional weapons were prohibited or restricted.
