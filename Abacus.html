Abacuses were a type of tool for performing mathematical calculations of sums and other simple operations that combined numbers, such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. It was not possible to perform complex equations upon them.

Description

An abacus consisted of a rectangular, wooden frame that typically measured around 8 inches (0.2 meters) by 10 inches (0.25 meters) across. Inside these frames were several heavy wires, tightly strung parallel to one another. Strung on each of these wires were typically ten wooden balls. By manipulating these balls, a user could perform simple mathematical operations.
