The Jewelers' Congress was a guildhall in Daggerford that was shared by the local Jeweler's Guild and the Whitesmiths' Guild.

Location

The guildhall was located at the junction of the Farmer's Road and Duke's Way in central Daggerford.

Structure

The building resembled something of a massive vault, akin to this that could be found within the walls of a bank. Each of the building's entryways were heavily reinforced and it well-guarded by both the local constables as well as the Daggerford militia .

Activities

In addition to being a meeting place for two guilds, the Jewelers' Congress served as a holding area for gems and precious metals.

Guild members could store their valuables within the guildhall, for a small fee based on the inherent value of what was being safeguarded.
