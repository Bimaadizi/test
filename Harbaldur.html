Harbaldur , nicknamed Baldur , was a dwarven blacksmith who saw the settlement that would eventually be known as Baldur's Gate , grow around his home and workshop on the river-mouth of Chionthar sometime before 0 DR .

History

Harbaldur was believed to be one of the first inhabitants of the town of Loklee, long before it would be known by other names. Harbaldur became well-known for his work forging grand, sturdy, yet ornamental castle and vault gates. As the dwarf traded and did business, the locals gave him a nickname – Baldur. With time, his business partners and new acquaintances began to believe that Baldur was his full name. Some historians believed that the name "Baldur" was adopted by the locals who began referring to the village of Gray Harbor as Baldur's Gate sometime before the 300 DR .

Centuries later, Harbaldur's name was overshadowed by the adventurer and investor Balduran , who became known as the founder of Baldur's Gate after returning from a profitable expedition and investing heavily in his hometown sometime in the mid-1000s DR.
