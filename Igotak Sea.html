Igotak Sea was the largest lake in the Nakvaligach region of the Great Glacier .

Geography

The Igotak Sea was a large triangular-shaped lake that was covered in thick ice, up to 10 feet (3 meters) thick, for most of the year. The lake itself was 40 feet (12 meters) in depth at its deepest point. The lakes murky water was drinkable but it was particularly bitter.

The lake was known for its ever-present fog. The thick fog covered the lake, rising several hundred feet. During the cold of winter, the fog rolled out and covered the entire area around the lake, to a distance of up to 50 feet (15 meters).
