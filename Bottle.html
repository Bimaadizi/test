Bottles were small liquid containers, generally larger than vials and flasks , though the terms were sometimes used interchangeably. Skins and canteens served a similar function, though usually just for water, while beakers were used for precise alchemical purposes.

Description

An idealized average bottle could hold 1.5 pt (0.71 L) of liquid. They were commonly made of glass or ceramic and stoppered with wax , leather , or cork .

Availability

Usage

Bottles were commonly used for valuable drinks like wine and potions .

Notable Flasks

Magical bottles contained special liquids, gases, and stranger things.
