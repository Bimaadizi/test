The Books of the Master Winemaker was an extremely valuable set of twelve books that detailed the art of growing, caring for, and harvesting of grapes and the fermentation of quality wine .

Content

Collectively, the twelve volumes were a complete guide to winemaking. The information within was detailed and guided the reader in selecting the land where a vineyard could be built, designed, and constructed; growing and tending to grapevines; and harvesting techniques. The tomes also detailed differences between varieties of grapes, the fermentation process, and various techniques and tools needed to produce the best-quality wines. Their guidance was detailed enough to offer a guide on tailoring flavors and aromas of the produced wine.

Being one of the most detailed guides to the art of winemaking, Books of the Master Winemaker was an excellent teaching tool. Diligent students could use the books to master the skill, given enough time and a winery with which to practice. They required a year of study and practice to become masters of the craft.
