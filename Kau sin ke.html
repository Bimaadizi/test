The kau sin ke was a weapon unique to the continent of Kara-Tur . It was also known as a whipping chain .

Description

It consisted of approximately four to six short iron bars interconnected by multiple links of chain, 4​ to ​6 feet (1.2​ to ​1.8 meters) in length. The weapon's origins were traced back to the agricultural flail . When wielded skillfully, it possessed the potential to be a formidable weapon, as the iron bars can encircle shields , delivering powerful and crushing strikes.
