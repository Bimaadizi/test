An aba ( pronounced : /ˈ ɑː b ɑː / AH-bah ) was a sleeveless, light fabric robe or cloak worn by men and women to protect against the elements, common to peoples from desert climes, such as Calishites , Zakharans , or the Bedine .

Variants

A heavier, more expensive type of aba , called a jellaba was worn in winter or at night.
