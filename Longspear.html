Longspears were a variety of spears noted for having a greater length and weight than the average spear .

Description

The length of the shaft for a longspear varied, though was generally in the range of 10​ to ​13 feet (3​ to ​4 meters), and weighed 8‒9 lb (3.6‒4.1 kg). They were built in a manner similar to pikes , another member of the spear family of pole arms that had considerably longer shafts.

Weaponology

Due to their considerable size and length, longspears were ineffective throwing weapons and required both hands to wield properly.
