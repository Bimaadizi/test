Sebben was a small town in the Shaar .

Description

The location was pleasantly pastoral, yet kept regular contact with the outside world.

Geographical Features

The city was built around a pond filled by a spring.

Trade

The town was the hub for sheep and goat ranches around the area. A local business bred and traded horses.

History

The town was founded by Otrur Sebben , who first put the road through the Rath Gap in the Rathgaunt Hills , connecting it to Shaarmid . The city was razed in the Year of the Bitter Root, 438 DR by the forces of Amahl Shoon VII during the Seven Burnings campaign.
