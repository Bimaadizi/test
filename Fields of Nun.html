The Fields of Nun was an open stretch of land running through the northern region of Chondath between the Vilhon Reach and the Akanamere .

Description

The Fields were fallow and tainted by the death and disease that had been inflicted there in times passed.

Geography

The Fields of Nun lay between the Nunwood and the northern end of the Chondalwood . The River Nun ran through it.

The Fields lay along the Trail of Tears , a pilgrimage route for the devout of Tempus . Pilgrims arrived from a one-way portal in Mistledale amid a ring of standing stones on a hill overlooking the river, followed the trail to an unnamed barrow on which nothing could grow because of all the diseased soldiers buried beneath, and proceeded through another one-way portal to a low hillock on the south bank of the River Thazarim in southeastern Thay .

History

The Fields of Nun was the site of an expenditure of Necromantic magic in Year of the Queen's Tears, 902 DR , that released a devastating plague upon Chondath during a civil war that was later called the Rotting War because of the disease.

In the Year of the Banner, 1368 DR , Garagos appeared near the ruins of the village of Gosra to destroy the imposter marilith Glackzana who had constructed a temple in his name.

Inhabitants

The fields were haunted by ghouls and ghasts that still carried diseases from the Rotting War, such as cackle fever and red ache .
