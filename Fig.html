Figs were edible fruits found in warmer climate nations of Faerûn and in further, among the nations of Kara-Tur and Zakhara .

Cultivation

Figs were known to be a sweet edible fruit as early as the ancient empire of Netheril .

Fresh figs were grown in the West Faerûn nation of Calimshan and often consumed with flatbreads along with prunes , dates , dried peas , and goat cheese .

In a similar fashion, in Sembia , figs were parts of feasts, served alongside other fruits and sweet-tasting meats, cheeses , white bread , butter , and pickled eggs . In the nearby kingdom of Cormyr , dried figs were available, namely, in the town of Marsember .

Dannath's Pickles, Nuts, & Foods in the city of Yartar sold dried southern fruits, including figs.

Just south of Riatavin in the Lands of Intrigue was located a rich Rhianna estate from which Thousandheads Trading Coster operated. The estate's grounds held massive acreages of fig and date orchards. Sunrise Orchards was another notable location that grew fig trees. Fresh figs were also a staple of Turmian cuisine.

Wild fig trees grew in the hot sands of Anauroch around the five Colored Waters lakes. The fruits were eaten raw, served with roasted hare , and dried to be eaten as rations.

In the southern land of Halruaa , sugared figs were a common sweet treat.

Sweet fig treats were served in Eltabbar for the Zulkirs and those who held power in Thay . Mulhorand and Great Vale cultivated fruits, including figs.

Figs were consumed by the Laska people of the Kilap Valley in Shou Lung during feasts of roast boar and fresh papayas .

People of the Haunted Lands in Zakhara cultivated dates, figs, and pomegranates . Figs were a commonly sold commodity in Tajar of the Pearl Cities , and grew in abundance on the isles of Masud Jazayir .

Usages

Calimshan was known for its figs, pressed into a flat oval shape – a treat simply called fig cakes .

In North Faerûn , honeyed figs were among some of the most common preserves, kept in wax-sealed jars .

Trivia

Calishites made a hand gesture called the fig sign by placing a thumb between the middle fingers. That sign was believed to ward evil names off.
