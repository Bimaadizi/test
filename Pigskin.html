Pigskin was the skin of pigs . It was also known as pork rind .

Uses

The skin of pigs was notably thick with fat , and could be fried and eaten as pork "cracklings", which were enjoyed in Cormyr .

Pigskin could be tanned to use as a material for making pouches —such as for coin purses or thieves ' tools—which was popular in the Border Kingdoms . Tanned pigskin was also used for upholstery—such as for cushions—although this was seen as a low status material. This was known to be used by swineherds in The Vast .

Pork rind was also used as a material component in the grease spell.

Availability

A 6‑square-inch (39‑square-centimeter) piece of pork rind intended for wizards ' spell component pouches sold for 3 copper pieces from Aurora's Emporium in the mid-to-late 14 th century DR .

At The Old Oyster restaurant in Monksblade , fried pigskin was used to encase their " old wyvern tail ", a dish meant to imitate wyvern meat.
