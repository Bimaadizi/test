Mamenchisaurus ( pronounced : / m ɑː m ɛ n tʃ ɪ ˈ s ɑːr ʌ s / ma-men-chi-SAR-us ) was a species of dinosaur .

Description

Mamenchisaurs were sauropods. Their massive body supported what is likely the longest neck of any natural creature found on Toril . Their neck is a full 35 ft (11 m) in length. Their neck contains nineteen vertebrae, which were supported by bony connections. This prevented the mamenchisaurus from bending its neck other than their head.

Combat

When targeted by a predator, mamenchisaurs used their tails as a destructive whip.

Habitat

Mamenchisaurs were found living in swamps.

Biology

Mamenchisaurs were herbivores and solitary creatures. Their long neck allowed them to reach the foliage from any tree.
