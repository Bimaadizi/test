Vellum was a high-quality writing surface material made from calfskin .

Description

Vellum was similar in form and function to other kinds of writing surfaces such as paper , parchment , and papyrus , though rarer and more expensive. It was made of animal hide, like parchment, but was made exclusively from the scraped and tanned hide of calves, producing a superior material.

Availability

Aurora's Emporium sold vellum sheets for 1 gp each, alongside various other writing surfaces and tools. It also sold 5‑inch (13‑centimeter) squares of vellum for 5 gp each, intended for use by priests in their rituals and spells.

In Waterdeep , the League of Skinners and Tanners handled all aspects of tanning and leatherworking, including the production of vellum.

Calaunt was a great producer and exporter of leatherwork including vellum.

Usage

Valuable projects such as building plans, academic treatises, and wizard 's tomes were often created upon vellum.

Vellum was an ideal material for creating spell scrolls , producing more reliable results than papyrus and parchment .

Detect poison used a strip of blessed vellum, turning black in the presence of poison .
