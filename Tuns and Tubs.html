Tuns and Tubs was a shop in the Central Trade Lanes of Skullport in the late 14 th century DR .

Structure

Tuns and Tubs was a one-story building in fair condition.

Services

The shop crafted a wide variety of durable containers, including basins, tubs, pitchers, and even bathtubs. Tuns and high-pressure fermentation vats, however, were its specialty, drawing brewers, vintners, and distillers from across the North who recognized the exceptional quality of the shop's products. Although the storefront appeared modest, prices reflected the superior craftsmanship that distinguished Tuns and Tubs in the region.

Inhabitants

Tuns and Tubs was operated by Marcus Vinlander . Despite his family's longstanding tradition in distilling, Marcus never successfully produced a proper batch of liquor . Instead, following a serious accident, an explosion that claimed his left arm, he discovered his aptitude for designing high-pressure fermentation vats.
