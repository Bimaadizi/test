Beregost Blue was a type of cheese produced in the town of Beregost in the Western Heartlands as of the late 15 th century DR .

Description

This cheese had a distinct tangy taste and blue streaks that crossed the cheese's body. If left to spoil, the blue streaks turned into green mold.

Usages

Beregost Blue was used to make a brown, almost gravy -like sauce to serve dumplings in.

History

Even though Beregost Blue was produced only in Beregost, passing merchants helped to export it all across the Sword Coast . Monks of Candlekeep were among the most loyal buyers of Beregost Blue, purchasing countless wheels over the years.
