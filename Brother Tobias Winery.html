Brother Tobias Winery was a locally favored winery in the city of Westgate on the Dragon Coast , on the crossing of Westgate Market Street and Brewhouse Lane .

Description

Brother Tobias Winery was a small multi-storied stone monastery-like building surrounded by a small public park with a small stream and a bridge. Underneath the winery was a wine cellar that extended into catacombs.

History

According to the winery's owner, it was, indeed, once a monastery, until its monks slaughtered each other over some disagreement. Subsequently, the monastery was purchased by Tobias Timble , who turned it into a winery, keeping its monastic theme.

By the late 14 th century DR , Brother Tobias' wines were a staple in many of Westgate's inns and drinking establishments, including Black Eye Tavern and Bent Mermaid Inn . Brother Tobias' 1368 vintage was considered an especially good year, making these bottles popular due to their quality and cheap price.

In the Year of Lightning Storms, 1374 DR , Brother Tobias Winery was closed to visitors due to renovations. However, that was just a cover story. The business owner, Tobias Timble, employed the monastery's old monks, who were turned into vampire spawn slaves, and the winery was run by the Night Masks , who accessed it via catacombs that led to Orbakh 's underground sanctum.

Inhabitants

After the monks were gone and the building changed owners, the winery's employees wore monk robes to keep with the theme.
