Ironfell rings , commonly called iron rings , were signature enchanted rings worn by the Warlock Knights and Luminaries of Vaasa .

Description

These rings were made of ironfell . Most looked no different than a plain iron ring (although perhaps with less luster and a somewhat crystalline structure), but individual Knights could customize their rings with jewels or runes. These rings were a symbol of a Warlock Knight's status and power.

Powers

As they were made of ironfell , these rings allowed the Warlock Knight to channel the power of Telos , however they could not be removed once the Warlock Knight had made their pact with the primordial . The rings additionally would grant the wearer premonitions of danger, which sharpened their senses and reaction times when faced with threats and made it impossible for them to be surprised by an enemy. Higher-ranking Knights were known to enchant their rings with additional magic.

The Ironfell rings also served as the foci for the Pact of the Iron Ring , a ritual in which two or more Knights swore oaths to each other that were unbreakable, lest the Knight incur the pactbroken curse . Performing this ritual called for the Knights to present their rings, which would begin to glow purple as the ritual commenced.

Ironfell rings were used by Luminaries in the raising of shardsouls .

History

By the late 15 th century DR , the Warlock Knights produced nonmagical versions of their iron rings that could be given to their servants and allies, thus enabling them to form a Pact of the Iron Ring with them. The Knights were also known to call for pacts and promises to be made on their rings with allies who did not possess a ring of their own, although the consequences of violating such an oath were unclear.
