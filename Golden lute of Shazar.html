Golden lutes of Shazar were a unique lutes that were enchanted by the music-loving aristocrat from Mulhorand named Shazar sometime before the late 14 th century DR .

Powers

Apart from dramatically improving the owner's musical aptitude, at least one of the golden lutes of Shazar allows the bard wielder to cast several spells . The cure serious wounds spell cast at a high proficiency level could be dispensed once per day, and the same went for a powerful version of a haste spell.

History

Ten golden lutes of Shazar were commissioned by Shazar long before the Year of Lightning Storms, 1374 DR , to serve as a gift for the pharaoh of the time. The pharaoh, a creature of musical taste and thunderous temper, was delighted hearing the ten golden lutes sing in the hands of master minstrels . Unbeknownst to the pharaoh, skill-busting enchantments were weaved into the instruments, allowing the minstrels to perform beyond their natural skills. Eventually, the ruler found out about the magic and took it as a great offense. Shazar and his minstrels were executed. Despite the incident, the pharaoh kept all ten lutes, and after his death, they were interred along with the pharaoh.

Many years later, the pharaoh's tomb was plundered, and the ten golden lutes of Shazar were scattered across Faerûn . In 1374 DR , one of the lutes belonged to a musician Cain Lethellon , who cockily challenged bards and entertainers to a lute-off in the Blacklake District of Neverwinter . Some said that the minstrel was beaten in his own competition by the famed Kalach-Cha or one of their companions, losing the Golden lutes of Shazar as a result.
