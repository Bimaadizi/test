House Akash was a mercantile house in the republic of Turmish in the mid-to-late 14 th century DR .

Organization

As was Turmishite tradition, House Akash was operated by the women of the family, with the man of the household, Akabar Bel Akash , merely serving as the family business's face. It was begun by a woman named Akash , but sometime after Kasim joined the marriage and made her own business a partner of their firm, with the co-wife became the chief manager of House Akash's operations.

The wives who ran the business had the power to veto any choice of additional wives that their husband Akabar might make.

Activities

This business primarily dealt in the trading of fine textiles and spices .

History

By the Year of the Prince, 1357 DR , Akash and Kasim were searching for a third sister-wife so that they could expand House Akash and have more money for the partnership.

That same year, Akabar Bel Akash traveled north as part of a trade caravan into Cormyr , delivering a shipment of fine textiles to Dimswart Manor in preparation for the wedding of Gaylyn Dimswart and Frefford Wyvernspur . On the way there caravan was attacked and wrecked by an ancient red dragon , Mistinarperadnacles Hai Draco , who snatched away one of the wagons carrying Akabar's spellbook and the halfling "bard" Olive Ruskettle .

In the Year of Shadows, 1358 DR , House Akash welcomed its newest sister-wife to the family endeavor, Zhara . When Akabar traveled north of Turmish that year, Akash and Kasim sent Zhara to accompany him so as to ensure he would be protected from "the barbarians of the north".
