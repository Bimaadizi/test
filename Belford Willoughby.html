Belford Willoughby was the son of Harvard Willoughby , a comedian and the host of the "Laff Riot" competition that was held at the Elfsong Tavern in Baldur's Gate in the late 15 th century DR .

Description

Belford looked a lot like his father. He a human boy with a light skin tone, blue eyes, blonde hair, and he was a snappy dresser, wearing a puffy beret decorated with a large quill feather.

History

In the Year of Three Ships Sailing, 1492 DR , Harvard Willoughby hosted a stand-up comedy competition called the "Laff Riot." Together with his young son, Belford, who was Harvard's less-than-enthused announcer, the duo occupied Elfsong's side room with a stage and a piano . Harvard would challenge guests to a stand-up competition of jokes in which the winner would be the one who causes the most cheering and applause. Harvard was the usual winner of the show, as the public seemed to enjoy the man's copious puns. He joked about drow comedian who used Tasha's hideous laughter on the attendees and that drow didn't need torches as they had darkvision . His other jokes included references to his wife who cheated on him with a mage who could cast mage hand all night long and that she ran away from him with a fiend , "fiend-zoning" the man. Harvard discouraged jokes about religion and appreciated good porcine humor.
