Giant mason wasps were huge versions of a common mason-wasp. They could be found in Zakhara and were viewed as messengers of the gods .

Description

Giant mason wasps had a jet-black exoskeletons and mandibles that glowed red with heat.

Combat

A bite from these creatures seared the flesh from because of the intense heat.

A giant mason waspâ€™s stinger injected a dangerous toxin that caused the victim to pass out and fever. Unconsciousness lasted for a few days and the fever could kill. Those who survived were permanentely weakened from the toxin.

All kinds of fire was useless against these creatures. They could shoot a cone of fire from their mouths several times a day.

Killing a giant mason wasp brought the evil eye upon the killer.

Ecology

These creatures served as protectors of good and opposers of evil in the wild and urban areas.

The females would mate once a month. After mating, the adult pair hunted down a large animal, paralyzed it, carried it back to her den, and the female would lay eggs on the creature. The female sealed the den to allow the eggs to hatch and feast in peace.

Habitats

In east Zakhara , a colony of these wasps lived within the Mosque of the Sacred Fountain in Dihliz . These wasps were even taught to speak midani by the priests of Zann who also lived in the mosque. Also in east Zakhara, these wasps could be found in the Genies' Garden .

Relationships

Giant mason wasps were friendly toward humans and demihumans and even made excellent pets or guardians. Vishap were considered to be their natural enemies because of their penchant for eating wasp eggs.

Usages

Many parts of a giant mason wasp were useful for magical items, but killing one was bad luck so most waited for the insects to die naturally. A potion of fire breath could be made from their fire glands. Once powdered, their exoskeleton could be used for a potion of fire resistance or oil of fire invulnerability . The venom from their stinger was used as a potent venom, but lost its effect after a week. It could also be used as a basis for antidotes.
