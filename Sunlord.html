Sunlords , or sunladies if female, were the specialty priests of Amaunator .

Culture

Amaunatori priests were mandated to learn the intricacies of the law of the land they lived in. The sunlords' legal knowledge reached extreme levels. Every sunlord knew the laws of the country, down to the individual provinces and cities they'd been raised and/or had lived in, which were not necessarily the same place. This knowledge could also extend to very obscure and/or foreign laws.

Every sunlord was literate and had a grasp of etiquette.

Abilities

Magical Abilities

Sunlords were spellcasting priests with an especially high aptitude for spells from the sun , law , and fire spheres. They had major access to the all , charm , fire, air , law , summoning , sun , and the time spheres , while they had minor access to the divination , earth , healing , necromantic , numbers , protection , and thought spheres .

With increasing strength, Sunlords developed ever more magical abilities. These included the ability to cast detect lie an increasing number of times per day. Abilities they could use once per day were rigid thinking , compulsive order , legal thoughts , and Sol's searing orb . Impeding permission could be cast once every three days. Sunray could be cast twice per day.

Although not every god's specialty priest could turn undead , Sunlords could do turn undead. However, they were not as good at it as their cleric brethren. This ability was shared even by evil sunlords, though they could no command them. This power was thanks to Amaunator's association with the sun , which was anathema to many undead , but also because these creatures were against the "laws of nature".

Sunlords could also get an idea about a person's ethical outlook.

Possessions

All sunlords were capable of wearing all kinds of armor and weapons as long as they were designed to bludgeon people.

History

Sunlords were the specialty priests of Amaunator. The name survived as the title of the leader of the Brotherhood of the Glorious Sun . Its holder in 1374 DR was Daelegoth Orndeir .
