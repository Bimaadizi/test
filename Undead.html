Undead were creatures that had died and then been animated by spiritual or magical forces. Some deities employed undead as divine servants; for example, the dwarven god Dumathoin used undead dwarves as divine messengers. Among the inhabitants of the Malatran Plateau , undead were known as the Headshrinker's children .

Abilities

Undead were extremely resilient to a number of effects and substances that were extremely harmful to the living. They were immune to all mind-affecting effects, poison , sleep effects, paralysis, stunning, disease , and death effects. In addition, it was not possible to drain their life force in any manner.

Origins

There were six basic ways a living creature could become undead:

Aquatic

Aquatic undead or marine undead were a subtype of undead that were better adapted to bodies of water and often risen from the bodies of those who had died in water.

Aquatic undead in the Realms included aquatic remnants , drowned ones or "sea zombies", drowned dead , lacedons or "marine ghouls", the velya strain of vampirism , and any aquatic animal risen from the dead, such as sharks . There also existed a special shark form of monster zombie known as death sharks . And though rare, there were aquatic variants of undead normally found on land, such as spectres , wights , and wraiths .

Some specialty priests of ocean deities in the Realms were granted a limited version of turn undead that could only affect aquatic undead. These included the Anchors of the Church of Umberlee and the Aquarians of the Church of Deep Sashelas . Though all manner of aquatic undead were also considered servants of the goddess Umberlee , who granted her priests the ability to call them up from the sea or create them in order to defend their temples or fight for them.

Psionic

Undead had a unique relationship with psionics . While they themselves were immune to mind-affecting powers or effects and mindfeeder weapons, effectively rendering half of a psionics user's arsenal null (especially that of a telepath ), so long as they had sufficient sentience to create a reserve of mental energy the undead had the capacity to wield psionic powers, including mind-affecting powers. Referred to as psionic undead , this rare subtype could arise from most forms of undead, such as skeleton warriors . Some psionic undead were creatures who had such powers innately in life, such as alhoons and vampiric mind flayers . Others were uniquely known for being psionic regardless of what the creature had been in life, such as the brain in a jar , caller in darkness , and psionic ashenwight .

Psionic undead were vulnerable to most of the same non-mind affecting powers or effects that specifically affected psionic creatures , though there existed some powers that could specifically target them. Detonate , which allowed a manifester to explode the body of a mindless corporeal undead (though not free-willed undead), and sever the tie , which could disrupt an undead's tie to the Negative Energy plane through psionic manipulation of planar conduits.
