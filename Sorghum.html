Sorghum was a grain cultivated as food during summer months. It could also be used as a sweetener.

History

During the invasion of Shou Lung by the Tuigan in the Year of the Serpent, 1359 DR , a decisive battle in the Ma' Yuan Province came to be known as the Battle of the Sorghum Field .

In the mid-to-late 14 th century DR , sorghum-derived sweeteners could be purchased for 3 silver pieces per 1 pint (0.47 liters).
