Silverfin was a term for varieties of fish found in the waters of Faer没n .

Availability

Silverfin were common in the rivers and oceans of Faer没n.

Central Faer没n

Large schools of whitefish were encountered in Sea of Fallen Stars , and the waters of the Dragonmere and the Neck that lay between Cormyr , Sembia , and the Dragon Coast .

Silverfin were also found in the Wyvernwater in Cormyr, and were a common catch of the 'mist fishers' of Immersea . In Immersea's fishpacking plants, silverfin were preserved in salt or oil and stored in kegs for transport and sale both within and beyond Cormyr. Silverfin and other fish were cultivated in the fishponds of the Royal Court in Suzail for meals at the Court and the Palace of the Purple Dragon , and in the mansions of Arabel .

Silverfin were also caught in the Moonsea . In cities like Hulburg on the north shore, Moonsea silverfins were cured in smoking-houses and there sold to traders. Moonsea silverfins would then be traded by fishmonger's wagons inland to places like Voonlar .

Sea of Swords and the Sword Coast

Silverfin was common in the Sea of Swords . Villages on the Sword Coast south of the Cloak Wood caught these drag-nets from small fishing boats. On the island of Mintarn in the Sea of Swords, tiny whitefish were farmed in breeding pools and fed with kitchen scraps.

They also caught by the fishermen of Kalathtyr , Amn , which lay at the mouth of the River Specie where it flowed into the Sea of Swords.

Silverfin, both saltwater and freshwater, were the most common seafood on offer in Waterdeep's fish markets, all year around. Felhaur's Fine Fish was a Waterdhavian fishmonger's that sold silverfin, among others.

Western Heartlands

The River Chionthar was another source of silverfin, and they were fished around Berdusk and Scornubel .

The North

In the Silver Marches , silverfin were almost the species found in the River Rauvin , River Surbrin , and lesser rivers of the region. The majority of human travelers here could tell a silverfin from a bluefin or other fish, and how to net or cook one simply.
