Bracers of defenselessness were cursed bracers that impeded the wearer's defenses.

Description

They resembled bracers of defense .

Powers

While they normally operated identically to bracers of defense , in response to a dangerous enemy, these bracers changed function from bolstering the user's defense to completely thwarting it. Once donned, these bracers could only be removed through the use of a remove curse spell or similar. By the Year of Wild Magic, 1372 DR , they were known to be tremendous hindrances, but no longer reported to thwart completely all of the user's defenses.

Versions described after the Spellplague were instead considered a specific property that was held in addition to whatever other capabilities a given device bore. In response to sufficient damage, the bracers' curse manifested for as long as combat continued, adding a notable hindrance to the user's defense and protection.

History

Per sages, the curse creating bracers of powerlessness came from a mispronounced word while the rite used to create other protective items was being devised; the faulty ceremony, with its mispronounced word, was still used by some, leading to the creation of these items down the centuries.

One of the wedding gifts from Tan Chin to Princess Tsenya Bhrokiti in the Year of the Helm, 1362 DR was a pair of bracers of defenselessness .

There was likely at least one set in a watery chamber of Undermountain , loaded with dead and well-decomposed bodies, in the 1360s DR.

Notable Owners

There were a pair of such bracers in the hoard of the dragon turtle Perizade .
