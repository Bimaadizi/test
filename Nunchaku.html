Nunchaku ( pronounced : /ˈ n ʌ n tʃ ɑː k uː / Nun-chah-koo ) were a type of exotic melee weapon typically used by monks .

Description

The nunchaku was made of two hardwood wands or dowels, 12​ to ​14 inches (0.3​ to ​0.36 meters) long and 1 in (0.025 m) in diameter, connected at one end by a stout leather cord or a metal chain.

Weaponology

It took special training, such as monks received, to use this exotic weapon effectively in melee combat.

Nunchaku could be used to disarm an opponent much like the flail . Monks could take full advantage of this quick, bludgeoning weapon.
