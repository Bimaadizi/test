A parang was a kind of machete from Kara-Tur .

Description

Parangs were machetes with heavy chopping blades and were about 2 feet (61 centimeters) in length and weighed around 4 pounds (1.8 kilograms). They were used as both tools and weapons.

A typical parang might sell for 15 yuan in a Kara-Turan market.

Reputation

Monks of Kara-Tur sometimes used parangs as weapons, and primitive people groups in Kara-Tur commonly carried the blades with them at all times. The race of creatures known as hsing-sing carried parangs on their raids.

Notable Owners

The Serpent , the enigmatic leader of the Phoenix clan of ninja , owned a parang of superb craftsmanship.

Kashigawa Yoichi , a kensai from Kozakura , was known to train students in the art of fighting with the parang.

Toipeye , a korobokuru bushi , owned a magical parang.

Awang Sophir , a famous human monk, fought with a parang.
