The Lapal Sea was a prominent freshwater lake found deep in the heart of the Chultan Peninsula .

Geography

The lake was found 100 miles (160 kilometers) inland from the coast and the Great Sea . It measured 100 miles (160 kilometers) in length and was slightly elongated from north to south. It served as the dividing border between the Mhair Jungles in the east and the Black Jungles in the west. Several rivers emptied into the Lapal Sea from the surrounding jungle including the River Amarast and a few unnamed rivers. The River Lapar flowed from the Lapal Sea's southern end, through the jungle to empty into the Great Sea at the Lapalgulph .

Activity

Boats were practically unheard of on the Lapal Sea since the yuan-ti of House Sseradess inhabiting the area preferred to swim and the other tribes rode harnessed elasmosauruses .

Inhabitants

The elasmosauruses dominated the Lapal Sea. Other creatures found here included ahuizotls , chuuls , and dark tentacles .
