Daerlun was a large city in Sembia .

Government

As of 1368 DR , the city was run by an elderly merchant named Halath Tymmyr , although he was a puppet for the evil commander of the guard, Allathast , who was a member of the Cult of the Dragon .

Circa 1480 DR , Daerlun came under the control of the Netherese Empire along with the rest of Sembia, but was annexed by Cormyr in the Year of the Quill, 1397 DR . The city declared itself a neutral independent city-state in the Year of the Silent Tear, 1439 DR , rather than be absorbed by Cormyr or stay under the direct influence of Sembia.

Defenses

Daerlun was the most heavily fortified city in Sembia, and boasted the tallest curtain wall on all of Toril . This 500‑foot-tall (150‑meter) wall was made of magically hard obsidian and was erected by the Netherese Empire in the Year of the Black Blazon, 1382 DR . It was garrisoned by a massive army of conscripts from across Sembia, who drilled on massive parade grounds near the city's markets.

Patrols out of Daerlun—mounted on flying griffons or veserabs —ranged as far as the Vast Swamp , the Thunder Peaks , and the southern Dales .

Trade

The city had a bustling marketplace and a wealthy trade quarter.

As of the mid-14 th century DR , Daerlunian linen sold for around 8 sp per square yard, while Daerlunian wool sold for between 9 sp and 2 gp per square yard depending on the weight of the fabric. The city imported such commodities as parchment , wine , and wool from Eveningstar in Cormyr.

History

The evil semi-religious organization, the Cult of the Dragon, had a presence in Daerlun as of the mid-to-late 14 th century DR, but preferred a secretive approach, using the city as a place to store armaments and to treat wounded members. The commander of the city guard, Allathast, used the elderly merchant Halath Tymmyr as a puppet to rule the city.

In the Year of the Awakened Sleepers, 1484 DR , the city prepared defenses against a possible Shadovar and Sembian invasion. Rumors abounded in the city about gods, war, and natural disasters.
