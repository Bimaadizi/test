Lizards were scaly reptilian animals that came in a variety of sizes, colors, and patterns.

Description

Lizards had a moderately elongated body, a tail that tapered down from their body width, and two pairs of legs held outward from the body. They were non-venomous usually palm-sized to two feet (sixty centimeters) in length, depending on species.

Ecology

Lizards were most abundant in warm climates with forested or jungle terrain, but lizards could be found most anywhere on land or underground except where very cold.
