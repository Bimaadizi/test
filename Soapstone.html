Soapstone , also known as steatite, was a soft and brittle but easily worked hardstone .

Description

This harder version of talc was found in colors ranging from white to green and was often dyed other colors. Easily worked by unskilled hands for low-detail carving, it could hold intricate patterns but was not hard enough to hold delicate shapes. It could be easily polished to a warm, glossy finish. A typical price was 9 sp per 1 pound (0.45 kilograms).

Powers

Colloquial wisdom gave soapstone some mystical properties involving fire or capturing warmth, but these have not yet been proven.
