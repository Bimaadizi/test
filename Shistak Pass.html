Shistak Pass was a mountain pass located in the Keryjek Ridge of the Great Glacier .

Description

The Shistak Pass was a man-nade pass, a cut extending into the Keryjek Ridge 10 miles (16 kilometers) from the Angalpuk side and 10 miles (16 kilometers) from the Alpuk side, with the remaining 20 miles (32 kilometers) in between incomplete. This has caused issues with adventurers and travelers, who found a dead end and were forced to turn around. Bandits from Eghagu took advantage of this and often ambushed these unfortunate travelers.

History

Construction on the pass began in 1284 DR when the residents of Hupiik and Isenghack realized a safe passage through the Keryjek Ridge would be advantageous for travel and trade.
