The Shrine of the Sheaf , later renamed Fox Tower , was a former keep turned into a shrine to Chauntea and later commandeered by bandits in the the mid–8 th century DR .

Location

The Shrine lay across Buckralam's Starn from the road that led into it.

Structure

The place was a former keep. It had a courtyard.

Interior

The feast hall was lit by candle wheels , and had a minstrels' gallery up top. Bedchambers lay beyond the hall, which also connected to the kitchen. Another passage led to the back of the keep, the pantries, and to Beldrun's Room , which had once been the abode of a priest and since been converted into furniture storage during the takeover by the Iron Fox. The keep had a front gate.

History

The place had been a keep originally, and was later converted to a temple of Chauntea . An old priest named Beldrum had once had a room there.

Around 755 DR , the bandit known as Iron Fox led his forces in seizing the Shrine for as a defensible position from which they could control The Starn . They had some minor repairs done; evicted the local priest, Rarendon ; and kidnapped any local woman they desired.

In the Year of Laughter, 761 DR , the Iron Fox's forces were broken and decimated by the combined efforts of the Chosen of Mystra Elminster Aumar and a local woman, Immeira . Nevertheless, it remained known as Fox Tower until at least 767 DR .
