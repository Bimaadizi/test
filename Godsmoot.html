The Godsmoot was a wide street that ran diagonally northeast-by-southwest through the Temple District in the city of Athkatla . Like the names of many streets in the temple district, the name "Godsmoot" hearkened to a time before the 14 th century DR when the ward of Athkatla was full of holy houses to gods like Azuth , Ilmater , and Tymora .

Geography

The northeast end of the street turned into Sarlannus Street , which verged northward, and the Street of Seven Altars , which continued northeast.
