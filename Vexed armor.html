The vexed armor was a unique breastplate that contained the essence of the glabrezu known as Vexing Thoughts .

Description

The vexed armor was a fiery enchanted plate—in reality, a demon that manifested itself as a set of armor of abyssal defense. The breastplate's front bore a malefic face; everything about its form and shape felt wicked yet meticulous.

Powers

The vexed armor was warm to the touch, making its magical nature quite obvious. When worn, the armor could not be removed unless a spell of remove curse was cast. The wearer received a boost to their constitution and gained complete immunity to all magical and mundane cold. In addition, the armor's protection was similar to that of a +2 enchantment, boosted the wearer's constitution, and allowed them to cast the fire storm spell once per day.

History

Many years before the Year of the Cold Soul, 1281 DR , the glabrezu known as Vexing Thoughts was summoned and bound to this breastplate. The summoner used the glabrezu's true name— Chalimandren —for the ritual. Subsequently, the name was forgotten, allowing Vexing Thoughts to kill the conjurer. By 1281 DR, the vexed armor was abandoned on the Burial Isle in the great northern lake of Maer Dualdon in Icewind Dale . The armor called to sentient beings and wanted to be worn. But those who were unwise enough to don the breastplate were forced into a battle of wills with the glabrezu and, eventually, killed. Only individuals who learned the creature's true name could have any hope of surviving wearing the armor and forcing the demon to submit to their will, with it serving as a powerful enchanted set of armor.

In the Year of Rogue Dragons, 1373 DR , Vexed armor ended up in the collection of enchanted items of Agaza the Dreamweaver, the night hag who plagued the town of Lonelywood with nightmares, before being driven out by the Hero of Neverwinter .
