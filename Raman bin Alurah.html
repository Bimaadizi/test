Imam Raman bin Alurah was a moralist priest of Najm and a prince of Hiyal around 1367 DR .

Personality

Raman was thin and tall. His gift for elaborate planning made him one of the Sultana's most dangerous sons.

Activities

As Imam, Raman led the religious following of Najm in Hiyal. He also served as the official keeper of Najm's mosque in the city. As a devoted moralist, Raman had close ties with the Pantheist League who hoped to instill their beliefs in Hiyal.

Relationships

Though not the youngest child of Alurah bint Asrah , he was her youngest son.
