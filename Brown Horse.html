The Brown Horse was a tavern in Thundertree in the 15 th century DR .

Location

The tavern was located to the northwest of the village, close to its entrance.

Structure

Its exterior was well-kept, even after the eruption of Mount Hotenow in 1451 DR . The intact, stony wall had a sign close to the entry door of a horse holding a tankard of ale.

The building was split into two sections: its eastern side housed the common room, whilst its western side housed the brewing canisters and kitchen.

Services

The Brown Horse was well known for it brilliant ale , and the tavern itself smelled of brewer's yeast .
