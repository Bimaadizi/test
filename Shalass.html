The shalass , sometimes referred to as the silver shalass , was a type of fish endemic to the North .

Description

The shalass bore a resemblance to trout , were covered in silver scales, and on average grew to be 2 feet (0.61 meters) in length.

Ecology

These fish were considered to be a delicacy throughout the North.

According to Volothamp Geddarm , they were best caught in a particular type of fish basket known as a cone net. However, expert fisherfolk were known to simply stab them with spears from a bank or raft.

Habitats

The shalass were primarily known to inhabit the Dessarin River . Though they could also be found in the Surbrin River and Laughingflow . Further north, they could be found in the Luruar region in the River Rauvin .
