Silverale Hall was a festhall , inn, and tavern in Athkatla , Amn .

Location

It was situated in Athkatla's Center District , a short 15-minute walk from Waukeen's Promenade .

Structure

Silverale Hall was a four-story building built from stone. The festhall's signage was a silver mug overflowing with brew.

Services

The festhall was noted for its competent kitchen and the good humor of its patrons, as well as for being remarkably well-kept and clean. Although popular, it never felt crowded. The clientele mostly consisted of vendors from Waukeen's Promenade, caravaneers, and various other locals.

History

Said to be one of the oldest establishments of its kind in Athkatla, Silverale Hall was still in business as of the Year of the Tankard, 1370 DR .
