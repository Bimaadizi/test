Waterdhavian cheese was pale with holes and came from Waterdeep , known as bowshot cheese in southern Waterdeep.

Geography

The cheese was available in most bars in the city and was also commonly eaten by the nobility.

Description

It had a sharp and tangy bite.

Prices

As of 1368 DR , a 1‑pound (450‑gram) wheel was around four silver pieces, and a 100‑pound (45‑kilogram) was available for around 35 gold pieces .
