The Order of the White Crane was a monastic order in Shou Lung .

Dogma

The sect was devoted to the Path of Enlightenment and to Shu Chia , one of the Nine Immortals and the God of Artificers and the Arts. It was a conservative sect, but nonetheless had a dogma of versatility.

Activities

In addition to their religious study and practices and their martial arts training, the White Crane monks trained as astrologers , craftsmen, herbalists, historians, and physicians. They focused on religious devotions, martial arts training, and manual labor in equal measure.

They freely allowed travelers to stay at their temple, but could share only a simple sleeping mat and vegetarian food, and anyone staying for more than a week was expected to work and earn their keep. Visitors were also permitted to attend their training.

Tactics

They practiced the White Crane style of kung fu , a martial art that emphasized swiftness and grace. Sohei of the order were skilled in wielding two matching weapons at once: butterfly blades , jitte , nunchaku , or sai .

Base of Operations

They were based in the White Crane Temple in the city of Kirin in Hungtse Province .

Members

Circa Shou Year 2607 (1357 DR) , the order was led by the abbot Ch'ang Tui , "Long Legs", who upheld its traditions.
