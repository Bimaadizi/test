Pork , often called boar in Faerûn , was the general term for the meat of pigs and boars eaten across the Realms. It was a popular focal point of human and dwarf cuisines from Faerûn and Kara-Tur , and was also found in halfling and Zakharan cooking.

Description

The meat of pigs and boars had a number of different names depending on the type of cut and whether the meat had been cured. Popular discrete cuts of pork included roasts, chops, loins, and ribs. Cured versions, usually achieved with salting or smoking, were durable foods that would not spoil over the winter or during long trips. These included salt pork , bacon , and ham .

Whole Pigs

Whole animals were generally eaten roasted, and were referred to as "roast pigs" or "roast boars". They were popular at inns and taverns and were often the focal point of festivals and feasts in places like Damara , the Moonshae Isles , and Shou Lung .

Roasts

In Faerûn, pork roasts were often scored to allow for cloves or garlic to be embedded in the meat during cooking, while in west Zakhara they might be flavored with cardamom . In places like the Vast , roasts were often paired with carrots , lentils , peas , parsnips , onions , or squash . In the North and the Eastern Heartlands , roasts were served with things like green vegetables , potatoes , parsnips, turnips , and radishes .

Salt Pork

Salt pork was a cured preparation common in the Faerûnean Heartlands , the Korinn Archipelago , the Lands of Intrigue , and the North. It was common as a staple during long winters and as rations for adventurers , sailors, and soldiers. Salt pork's durability made it popular in places where pigs could normally not be raised, such as in the Underdark . Salt pork generally sold for about 3 gold pieces per 1 pound (450 grams).

Bacon

Bacon was a cured preparation generally found in northern and western Faerûn, and it was particularly common as part of a dawnfry or morningfeast . Bacon generally sold for about 4 gold pieces per 1 pound (450 grams).

Ham

Ham was a cured preparation usually made using the meat from the leg or hock. Ham generally sold for about 5 gold pieces per 1 pound (450 grams).

Stews

Pork was featured in hearty stews across Faerûn, paired with other meats, and with vegetables like onions, leeks , and greens as well as with spices . Boar meat was used in wildsage stew , eaten by farmers in the Vast.

Mince

The meat was sometimes eaten minced, often as a filling for dumplings, pies , pastries, and other dishes. Minced pork was one of the three meats most commonly found in a baeranth pie, in " jack wyvern tail " (meant to mimic the flavor of wyvern meat), and in tavern "steak" . It was also a key ingredient in Delzoun meatballs, known as "tide me overs" .

Sausage

Everything from fresh pork to pork fat to pigs' brains were used to make sausage . Often, these might be mixed with various other meats or other additions like nuts . Pig bladders were sometimes used as sausage casings. Pork sausage generally sold for about 1 gold piece per 1 pound (450 grams).

Other

Pork fat was used for cooking and baking. Rancid pork fat was boiled into a stew and fed to prisoners in Raven's Bluff . The fatty skin of the pig ( pork rind ) could be fried to make "cracklings".

In parts of northwest Faerûn , pig organs were used to make gravy and pig joints were eaten doused in gravy.

Pig's heads were a delicacy in Amn .

Availability

Pork was eaten across Toril , from dumplings eaten by the poor folk of Amn to suckling pigs at the tables of the nobility in the Cold Lands to roasts in the Pearl Cities of Zahkara to steamed buns known as " bow " in Kara-Tur to boar dishes in Laerakond . It was bought and sold, both fresh and cured, in city markets like those in Ravens Bluff and Arabel .

Pork was a common food at inns in Faerûn, and was often served spicy in the south (such as in Tethyr ) and more mildly seasoned in the north (such as in Waterdeep).

Cured preparations of pork could be bought in bulk, and owing to their longevity, they were popular even in places where fresh pork was hard to acquire, such as in Skullport and Calimshan . Salt pork was one of the most popular exports out of Featherdale , where it was famously cured with minerals from the Deeping Stream .

History

Pork was a staple food in north Faerûn since the days of ancient Netheril .

High demand among the nobles of Chessenta made pork one of their most important imports as of the mid-to-late 14 th century DR owing to the fact that few pigs were raised in the realm itself.

In the late 15 th century DR, the merchant Alsimur Droon manufactured a shortage of salt pork along the Sword Coast , allowing him to capitalize on the desperate demand.

Trivia

The flavor and texture of pork was said to be similar to the meat of wyverns, rothé , and subterranean lizards . Pork when flavored with chives was also said to simulate the taste of stirge meat.

Pork was one of the favorite foods of the average beholder . Perytons were not over fond of it.
