The le Shay were immortal, albino, elf -like fey creatures of extraordinary magical power. They were said to be the progenitor race of the fey, and as such were considered the rulers of the plane of Faerie since ancient times. They were sometimes referred to as sylvans or simply as the fey .

Description

Le Shay were legendarily beautiful. They were tall and thin and looked like albino elves, with white skin and glossy dead-white hair. However, instead of the red eyes common to true albinos, le Shay had deep black eyes; even the whites of their eyes were black. Their ears were pointed, but more closely resembled the ears of a half-elf .

Personality

A le Shay typically was haughty and very concerned with proper behavior, manners, and mores. They cared strongly about matters of respect.

Abilities

Le Shay were immortal; they could not die of old age or illness but only be killed by someone.

Le Shay had a vast array of magical powers inherent to themselves. Most notable among such powers were their ability to charm other creatures by simply looking at them and the strange power of forming weapons out of nothing by power of thought alone. These "le Shay weapons" were formed from the essence of each individual le Shay as if they were extensions of his or her own body. Le Shay could also change form , teleport , turn invisible , and breathe underwater with their magics, among other things.

The minds of le Shay were particularly geared towards the learning of language; that is, all le Shay were essentially polyglots . They could understand nearly any language within only moments of hearing it spoken.

Combat

Despite their great power—on par with the oldest of dragons —le Shay tended to avoid initiating combat. They were superb fighters, but they preferred to act on the defensive.

Society

Le Shay were solitary and could survive in any climate and terrain. They preferred to make use of temporary structures (such as pavillions ) rather than build permanent buildings. They were said to worship the faerie gods .

Many le Shay were highly accomplished and dangerous spellcasters. They were known to be masters of portal magic and of inter-planar travel.

They spent most of their time in their immortal lives attempting to occupy their attention and amuse themselves. Their endless lifespans meant that they could not generally recall the details of their childhoods, and the many years ultimately could rob them of any subtlety or passion save for their pride. This eternity-induced apathy extended to their culture: everything from their wine to their fashion to their taste in music was bad by human standards. Their idea of fine dining included force-fed ortolans drowned in brandy and roasted whole.

Despite their eternal lifespans, le Shay females did not tend to bear more than one or two children owing to low fertility and yearslong, dangerous pregnancies. Generational differences within a family meant little to the endless le Shay, whose familial bonds grew strained and strange over the eons: knowing that ultimately their fellow le Shay were the only ones who could always be there for them, they came to both hate and cling to each other. Inheritance and succession favored the youngest rather than the eldest child.

Uses

The amber-colored blood of a le Shay was said to be so rare and potent that it made for a powerful component in dark summoning rituals .

History

The le Shay were said to be the progenitors of all fey, with some believing them to even be the originators of the elves. If true, this meant that they were responsible for the creation of beings like the korreds , sprites , and pixies , and that they ruled Faerie as of −34,000 DR . While they were said to have some civilization on Toril during this time , they largely kept to secluded woodlands, living in harmony with nature and not competing with the empires of the sarrukh , batrachi , or aearee . They had largely retreated back to Faerie as of −31,000 DR , and those who remained on the Material Plane became scattered, with some scholars believing that this was a result of a plague brought about by dragons or demons . It was said that the descendants of those who stayed behind became further types of fey.

Circa −27,000 DR , they opened new portals from Faerie to Faerûn to allow the ancient sylvan elves to immigrate there.

In the millennia after the First Sundering , the High Lady Ordalf led the le Shay in founding the kingdom of Sarifal circa −10,500 DR on the island that would one day be known as Gwynneth of the Moonshae Isles . Some believed that it had been the le Shay's influence through the preceeding 500 years that ended the volcanic activity of the islands and caused them to take on their final shapes. From their shining capital city of Karador , they ruled over the many fey inhabitants of the island and subsequently welcomed high elven refugees circa −9800 DR , who would become the Llewyrr .

Around −4000 DR , the le Shay were said to have allied with the Imaskari , and to have taught them planar magics that would one day allow the Imaskari to enslave the ancestors of the Mulan people from another world . And in 1374 DR , Ususi Manaallin found an image of seven le Shay, each carrying a tome, in Imaskaran ruins, prompting her to wonder if these were the "more-than-mortal" creatures that helped make the Imaskarcana .

The le Shay would subsequently retreat from Toril circa −500 DR , traveling to the Feywild and causing Karador to sink to the bottom of the Myrloch lake. They returned in dramatic fashion in the Year of Risen Elfkin, 1375 DR , causing Karador to reemerge and beginning the process of driving all humans from the island of Gwynneth as they reestablished their realm of Sarifal.

As of 1479 DR , only a handful of le Shay yet lived on Toril, all of whom were immensely powerful spellcasters residing in Karador in Sarifal on Gwynneth island. Most of the le Shay were reclusive, rarely seen even by most of the city's populace, and served as advisors to the less-reclusive High Lady Ordalf and her son Prince Araithe , who governed the city.

As of the late 15 th century DR , some elven crusaders who revered Lashrael opposed the influence of the le Shay of Sarifal.

LeShays of Sarifal

While there were many families of le Shay in the Feywild, as of the late 15 th century DR, the ruling le Shay of Sarifal all hailed from the same family, and occasionally used "le Shay" as a family surname. They were cruelly protective of the purity of their bloodline, which led to a great deal of inbreeding, ultimately resulting in several stillborn or monstrous births. The family's royal sigil was that of a climbing rose which members had tattooed on their bodies, including Princess Amaranth (in yellow), Princess Callia (in white), and Queen Ordalf (in pink).
