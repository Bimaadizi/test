Cloudberries were a species of raspberry endemic to the High Moor region.

Description

Much like other raspberry species, the fruits of the cloudberry bush grew on long, whip -like and wiry branches that were known as canes. But unlike the standard raspberry, these fruits had a yellowish hue.

Much like the bilberry bush, their canes tended to form dense, interwoven thickets that made it challenging to retrieve these berries.

Usage

Said to be very palatable, cloudberries were a staple in the diet of many animals that inhabited the High Moor plateau, with even humans and goblinkin known to forage for them.
