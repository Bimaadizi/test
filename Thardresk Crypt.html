Thardresk Crypt , also known as the Dungeon of Swords , was the supposed family tomb of the royal House Thardresk , ruling family of the ancient kingdom of Anauria . It was said to be the final resting place of the great King Thausimbel , as well as his daughter Queen Shaddara .

Geography

The crypt was one of many of its kind dating back to Anauria, set within the center of the Serpent Hills , in the Western Heartlands .

It served as a portal nexus that opened a network of portals between a number of Anaurian tombs, including another false Thardresk family crypt beneath a ruined temple of Jergal in the eastern deserts of Anauroch , and an empty tomb beneath the Evermoors .

Description

Circa the late 14 th century DR , the crypt was only accessible by means of a tunnel that connected to the carved-out pathways of a mining expedition. All other openings had been sealed away by an earthquake some time ago.

It was as heavily guarded as the other tombs in the area, with magical traps and tricks, along with undead , ready to attack adventurers.

History

In the Year of the Gate, 1341 DR , gnomes of Clan Argentum began digging into the center of the Serpent Hills in search for silver and broke through into the crypts. A few of the braver gnome warriors ventured within but were forced to flee and abandon their dig site in short order.

Starting in the Year of the Unstrung Harp, 1371 DR , agents of the Cult of the Dragon and members of House Orogoth both sought out the crypts for their own ends.

Inhabitants

At least one adult deep dragon was known to dwell within the deepest depths of the crypt before being slain by dragon cultists circa 1371 DR , though its mate survived to drive them out.

Rumors & Legends

Throughout the history of its existence, rumors held that it was actually a false crypt, disguising the existence of a number of tombs for the royalty of Anauria that were linked together by its portal nexus .
