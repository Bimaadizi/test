The Farmer's Gate was one of three entry points into the city of Daggerford in the Western Heartlands in the mid-to-late 14 th century DR .

Location

The Farmer's Gate provided access to the city from the north. The gate was located at the junction of the Farmer's Road and Wall Street .

Activities

The gate was usually left open even at night. It was Daggerford's busiest gate given its proximity to the fields.

Defenses

The gate was only accessible after crossing a moat and bridge. The road leading through the gate passed beneath a single guard tower. The Farmer's Gate stood 10 feet (3 meters) tall.
