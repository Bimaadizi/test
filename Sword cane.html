Sword canes were a type of concealed weapon common in urban settings.

Description

These canes appeared to be expensive and well-crafted walking sticks with shafts made of some type of hardwood, usually hickory or oak . Concealed within this shaft would be a long, stiletto -like blade whose hilt acted as the cane's handle. These hilts were typically made of gold , ivory , or silver .

History

A several hundred years prior to the 14 th century DR , these became quite popular among high society in the Turmish city of Alaghôn . They were ornate and adorned with ivory, gems , feathers, and ornate metal finecastings.

By the mid-14 th century DR, only a few members of high society in Alagôn still clung to this fashion, with many of their previous owners having had the canes buried with them. Around that time they were also beginning to become of interest to noblemen and thieves in Sembia .
