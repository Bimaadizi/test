Mystra's Dance was an island at the mouth of the Delimbiyr River .

Geography

The isle was part of a delta formed out of bare islets , but the isle itself had at least two hills, one of which held an altar.

Geographical Features

There was a stone pier on the shore; a hill separated the pier from another hill, atop which was a dark, circular featureless altar.

History

Around the turn of the 230's DR , the Mystran priestess Elmara (secretly prince Elminster Aumar of Athalantar ) was bid by the mad mage of Calishar , Ilhundyl , to deliver Ondil's Book of Spells to the altar on Mystra's Dance.

In Year of Much Ale, 233 DR , she completed the task. Upon her placing the book on the stone, the ghostly hands of a lich pulled the book away, and an apparition of a woman twice her height in a flowing gown appeared; Elmara followed her from the hill and into the waters, where she found herself capable of treading. At a certain distance from the shoreline, a voice called out "you have failed me", and her water walking was stopped, making her plunge into the icy depths.

She survived. Not long after, she petrified and teleported several of Seldinor Stormcloak 's apprentices, who had just gotten stabbed in a barfight at the nearby Unicorn's Horn tavern, to the same waters where she almost drowned.

By the 14 th century DR , the mouth of the Delimbiyr had become the Lizard Marsh .

Rumors & Legends

In the early-to-mid 3 rd century DR , it was said people who spent overnight in the island were subject to a number of maladies, some of them deadly.

The island was named as such because it was believed to hold the first of all altars to Mystra.
