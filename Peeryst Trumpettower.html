Peeryst Trumpettower was a nobleman of Hastarl in Athalantar in the 3 rd century DR .

Description

During his wedding, he wore a high-plumed hat, and a cloth-of-gold doublet. He normally wore bell-trimmed hose and curl-tipped shoes.

Possessions

Peeryst had received for his wedding, from the bride's father, an immense diamond carved into the shape of a stag , which was placed in his bridal chamber. Vigorous activity in the room shattered it to pieces on its first night.

Relationships

He was married to Nanue Glarmeir . His uncle was Darrigo Trumpettower .

History

As part of his marriage to Nanue Glarmeir in the Year of the Black Flame, 229 DR , Peeryst paraded past the temples of Sune , Lathander , Helm , and Tyche , completing the circuit under the sword of Tyr . He also received a statue known as the Stag of Athalantar. However, shortly after the toast at their bridal chamber, the newlyweds were attacked by the Moonclaws gang, and a fight resulted as they clashed with the Velvet Hands gang, which also tried to burgle the couple on their bridal night. Only the intervention of Darrigo Trumpettower finally evicted the thieves, though they caused much damage to their possessions in the meantime.

Later in the Year of the Chosen, 240 DR , Peeryst Trumpettower was in Athalgard with his wife during the invasion of the forces under Prince Elminster Aumar , who sought to overthrow his uncle Belaur Aumar and stop the Magelords of Athalantar who ruled for him. As Peeryst thought to check what the alarm was about, a soldier ran into him, making him fall and break his nose against the wall. As his wife came to check on him, fellow nobleman Jansibal Otharr , scrambling to his post, injured his wife, cutting into her arm with the sword he had out. His uncle decided to take the fight to Jansibal, took Peryst's blade, and told him to bind Nanue's wounds.

Rumors & Legends

It was said that Darrigo Trumpettower had hurled all of the Moonclaws thieves out of his nephew's bridal suite while the newlyweds consummated without noticing the tumult, which was an embellishment.
