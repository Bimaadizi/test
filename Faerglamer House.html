Faerglamer House was the residence of Bilnur Faerglamer through the late 9 th and throughout the 10 th century DR .

Location

The house was in Tethyr .

Interior

During Bilnur's stay, the house was inhabited by him alone, with piles of coins abandoned across the floor; birds nested freely inside the abandoned rooms. The outside of the house remained overgrown and somewhat dilapidated. After he died, the house slowly turned into a ruin, broken open to the sky.

Activities

Bilnur Faerglamer lived there and used it as a workshop.

Defenses

While Bilnur lived there, he had a number of guardians, probably variants of helmed horrors with more power than normal; those melted apart after his death.

History

Prior to the Year of the Curse, 882 DR , the house had been built for a then-extinct family. Bilnur Faerglamer lived in the house from around 882 DR , the better part of seventy years prior to 952 DR , to 977 DR , the year of his death. After his death, the house decayed into a ruin, with the coinage stashed there soon taken.

Rumors & Legends

Though the coins that Bilnur hoarded had been taken long since, some mages of the late 14 th century DR still expected to find a cache somewhere in the ruined and overgrown manor. However, there were rumors of often invisible shapechangers hunting visitors to the ruins.
