A sap , also called a blackjack , was a type of light, nonlethal melee weapon used for knocking people unconscious.

Description

A sap was filled with lead shot (small pellets), sand , or a similar material.

They weighed about 2 pounds (910 grams).

Weaponology

Saps were designed to deliver non-killing blows, usually to knock someone unconscious. The act of trying to knock someone out with a sap was referred to as "sapping".

Varieties

Notable Users

Rogues and bards were often proficient with saps.
