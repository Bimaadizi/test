Javelins were a type of simple, thrown, ranged weapon, similar to a shortspear .

Description

Javelins were light, flexible spears designed primarily as a throwing weapon, but were equally suitable for melee. They were usually constructed of wood and tapered to a sharp point at one or both ends. Alternatively, they could be tipped with a small metal spearhead. The shaft was much thinner than a spear intended for melee combat.

Weaponology

The javelin was a ranged weapon, intended to be thrown. Javelins were not sturdy enough for most melee combat and if wielded in this way they would perform poorly. The normal maximum range of a javelin was 150 feet (46 meters).

Javelins could easily be used on both foot or mountedback.
