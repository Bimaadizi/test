Malachite was an ornamental stone related to azurite that was often used in inexpensive jewelry and was beloved among the dwarves .

Description

Malachite was distinguished by its green stripes of many shades. Striking in appearance and inexpensive, these ornamental stones were usually tumbled smooth or cut cabochon and polished for jewelry among those that could not afford fancier gems. A typical specimen had a base value of 10 gp .

Usage

Flecks of malachite were used by the hag Auntie Ethel in the creation of the Heart of Stone potion. The concoction offered significant protection against all manner of poison .

Halfling pottery from Luiren created between 750 DR and 830 DR was known to be intricately decorated with malachite and lapis . This was somewhat unusual as hin preferred functionality to decoration.

Powers

Malachite was a spell component for some divine spells of the elemental sphere and arcane spells that dealt with cold, heat, ice, or fire. It was crushed into powder for use in magical immersions for items that promoted warmth, cold resistance, or fire resistance. For the same reason, bowls of commanding water elementals were crafted out of malachite or jade. Powdered malachite was also used to empower the contact other plane spell and reduce the chance of the caster going insane in the process.

Rumors and Legends

Malachite was reputed to prevent falls and therefore was often used on devices like rings of feather falling as part of the adornment. However, this was just catering to public demand because malachite had no gravitational intervention powers.
