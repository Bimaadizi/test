Laothkund was a city in the Unapproachable East , on the coast of the Sea of Fallen Stars . It was later known as Laothkund the Drowned after it sank into the sea.

Geography

There were caverns beneath Laothkund, which the merro sons of the great shaman Porag wanted as part of their reign.

Government

Circa 764 DR , Laothkund had a number of lords.

The independent city had been under Thayan control since 1357 DR . However, they held the city only nominally as of 1368 DR , and loosely as of 1372 DR . Like much of the Wizards' Reach , the Shadowmasters of Telflamm had strong influence there.

A ruling council existed on 1375 DR , which the Shadow Tongue thieves had plans to blackmail by implicating them in false conspiracies with the Red Wizards , and slave trade in children (which was illicit).

History

The region was first settled by people from Chessenta between −250 DR and −100 DR . The city of Laothkund broke free of Unther in 482 DR . Unther abandoned its wars on 679 DR to focus on reclaiming Laothkund.

In the late Year of Mistmaidens, 764 DR , the armies of the Lords of Laothkund struck at Nethrar (later Nethra ) in Galadorna , destroying the Unicorn Kingdom forever.

Laothkund was important to Gargauth 's worship.

The city came under Thayan control in 1357 DR , during the Salamander War . During the Spellplague of 1385 DR , Laothkund's Thayvian government was secured and reinforced by the forces of Mordulkin . They city became Laothkund the Drowned at most a decade later, by 1396 DR , after the seismic shifts from the Spellplague caused the land to sink beneath the waves.

By 1479 DR , the submerged and silted streets were populated only by fish and sea monsters. Much of the city's old wealth had yet to be plundered.

Demographics

Half-elves of wood elf stock were very common in the city. Like in the rest of the Wizards' Reach , the humans were a grand majority Mulan , with a smaller number of Chondathans and Rashemi , and a small fraction of Damarans .

Weather

Gusts from the Sea of Fallen Stars usually kept the city fairly temperate, even in Midwinter .

Notable Locations

Sal's Tavern

Sal's tavern was a location close to the Shadow Tongue lair. The local sentries were known to go there.

Tannery

The tannery was a quarter with narrow streets.

Smokehouse Inn

This inn served alcohol, and included locations for armwrestling.

Shadow Tongue Lair

The lair of the Shadow Tongue thieves' guild was on a building at least three stories tall, with one window on the third story boarded up on the outside and brick-and-mortared from the inside. The room that bore that window had been converted to a storage closet; down the hallway, there were rooms, a downwards-pointing stair with a switchback, and on the hallway below, at least one office and one privy. A hidden panel led to another set of stairs, and an iron-valved door that led to the vault, which doubled as Sathra's personal quarters.

In the vault, objects of great value were stored. Two smokeless torches were placed on stone pillars flanking the entrance; the shade of the pillars kept the back of the room dark. The obsidian tiles of the vault helped keep the room dark. There was a fabled red room somewhere else in the lair, where Sathra could visit unlikely tortures on her foes.

Green Warrior Inn and Tavern

This tavern featured some women of the night.

Tenambulum's Library

The mage Tenambulum had a library in the city as of 1374 DR . There were wards in it.
