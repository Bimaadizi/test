Morfred was the architect of Moonrise Towers in the Western Heartlands in the mid-to-late 14 th century DR , when he served Ketheric Thorm . However, Morfred turned against him following his conversion to Shar and purge of Selûnites . Morfred and his brother Halfred formed a secret Selûnite resistance against Ketheric, which found only futility until the cambion Raphael offered him a deal: Morfred's soul for the extermination of Ketheric's Dark Justiciars . In death, he was known as the Infernal Mason and one of the many debtors that existed in Raphael's House of Hope .

History

During the 14 th century DR , Morfred was a servant of General Ketheric Thorm , and was commissioned by him to construct Moonrise Towers in honor of Selûne . Morfred readily agreed, being himself a devout follower of the Moodmaiden. However, Melodia Thorm , Ketheric's wife, soon passed, and was shortly followed by Isobel , Ketheric's daughter. In his grief, Ketheric spurned Selûne and turned to Shar , building up an army of Dark Justiciars to eradicate Selûnite sympathies and kill those who did not convert, Morfred included. Morfred, in his log, detailed Ketheric's fall from grace and speculated that Shar weaponized his grief for her benefit, as well as implying that Ketheric only converted to Selûne because of Melodia's influence.

By 1373 DR , Morfred and his brother, Halfred, formed a secret Selûnite resistance despite investigations from Sharran forces. Halfred went to the Last Light Inn , while Morfred formulated a holdout in the basement of the Reithwin Mason's Guild , where he once worked, and planned to gather allies to oppose Ketheric. Morfred feigned allegiance to Shar, but on several occasions upon drinking too much, his true loyalties came out, once in front of Thisobald Thorm , who reported his findings to Ketheric. Some time after, the Mason's Guild was raided by Sharrans, resulting in Halfred assuming his brother was dead if not already captured.

Noticing Morfred's desperation against the Sharrans, the cambion Raphael approached Morfred and offered him a deal: Morfred's soul for the eradication of Ketheric's army. Morfred accepted, allowing Raphael to enlist the orthon Yurgir to annihilate the Dark Justiciars, save for one, Lyrthindor , whom Raphael also approached to keep Yurgir bound to his contract. Without his army and routed by the Emerald Enclave and Harpers , Ketheric, in his defeat, laid a shadow curse on the land . Any individuals who were caught within the curse turned to shadow-cursed undead, save for Moonrise Towers, owing to Morfred's precautions of safety. At some point afterwards, Morfred died and, as per his contract with Raphael, was bound to the House of Hope as a debtor. Morfred's bones were put on display within the Mason's Guild for all to see.

The Infernal Mason

Morfred, having forgone his original name, was instead known only as the Infernal Mason, and served Raphael. In the Year of Three Ships Sailing, 1492 DR , the House of Hope was visited by a group of adventurers seeking the Orphic Hammer . They encountered Morfred, who gave vague answers about the nature of the House, but mentioned his previous accomplishments of being the architect behind Moonrise Towers. When told of Thorm's demise and the lifting of the shadow curse, Morfred expressed delight that eventually his actions led to Ketheric's fall, but lamented that it had come too late for his family and friends. He then urged the adventurers to escape the House while the opportunity presented itself.
