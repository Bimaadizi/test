Castle Obarskyr , also known as the King's Castle , was the royal palace located in Suzail , Cormyr .

Location

The castle was located on a dominating hillock , separated from the rest of the city by Lake Azoun , the Royal Gardens , and several castles of lesser nobility. King Duar Obarskyr , in the 5 th -century DR , called it Helmstar Castle, which he claimed had come to him in a dream.

Structure

The royal wing of the castle was decorated with purple carpets. The castle's court was renowned for the small savory pastries that were made in its kitchens.

History

Faerlthann's Keep

Faerlthann's Keep was the original stone tower on the hillside in Suzail and the historical seat of power in Cormyr . It was named after Faerlthann Obarskyr , the first king of Cormyr. It was built up from the original house that stood on the spot. During the reign of Boldovar the Mad , the windows of the keep were turned into barred slits.

Noble Court

In the years leading up to the Year of the Leaping Hare, 376 DR , the aristocrats and bureaucrats had gradually been thrown out of the keep itself, and had formed a collection of stone buildings further down the hillside, known as the Noble Court, or simply the court.

Attack by Thauglor

In the Year of the Dracorage, 1018 DR , Thauglor , a huge black dragon , attacked the castle and demolished around a quarter of it, setting fire to the remaining structure and causing extensive damage. Thauglor withdrew before the military could launch a response to his attack.

Interior

Inhabitants

By the late 15 th century DR , the Ormantrels , the Saraclarrs , the Lionwinters , the Bracetels , the Duskroses , were among families who served the Crown of Cormyr and resided in the Cormyr's Royal Court south of Castle Obarskyr. Members of the Court families were career officers and chamberlains for multiple generations.

Rumors & Legends

It was rumored that the royal treasury contained great riches protected by magic, monsters and traps.
