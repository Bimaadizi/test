A buzuq ( pronounced : / b ə ˈ z uː k / buh-ZOOK ) was a stringed Zakharan instrument that was comparable to an elongated lute .

Description

The buzuq's metallic strings were stretched over a long fretted neck connected to a wider, nearly circular body.

History

The buzuq was one of the favored musical instruments of the wandering tribes of Al-Badia , particularly in the southern regions of the Land of Fate.
