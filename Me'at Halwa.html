The Me'at Halwa was a lake in Raurin . After the pharaoh Amun-Re died, his curse dried it out; the fight between Martek and Khalitharius filled its dry banks with molten glass, rendering it part of the Skysea .

Overview

The Me'at Halwa was formed by the waters from five surrounding small hills. Much of the city of Medinat Muskawoon was built on them, and around the lake. The lake emptied into the river Athis , to flow northwestward and eventually reach the sea.

History

The city of Medinat Muskawoon was built around the Me'at Halwa. After the Curse of Amun-Re took effect, the level of the underground waters, themselves fed by the River Athis, fell enough that the outflow from the surrounding hills was not enough to keep the water in place. Thus, the lake dried, even though it was not fed by the river Athis.

As of 1356 DR , the Me'at Halwa was a part of the Skysea, created after the battle between Martek and Khalitharius. The lake's dry bed, and part of its banks leading towards the Athis, were covered in a layer of glass with an average thickness of 10 ft (3 m).
