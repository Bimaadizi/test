Coke was an uncommon form of highly refined fuel distilled from coal or petroleum .

Properties

Coke was a gray, carbon -rich mineral-like substance that burned readily, with similarities to charcoal . It was about as heavy as coal, weighing between 62 pounds (28 kilograms) to 105 pounds (47.6 kilograms) per 1 cubic foot (28 liters).

Availability

Coke was normally processed from coal in an oven, but could also be found naturally as "igneous coke" when underground heat and pressure acted on underground deposits.
