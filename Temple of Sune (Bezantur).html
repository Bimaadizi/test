The Temple of Sune in Bezantur , Thay , was a place of worship dedicated to Sune that was popular with the locals as a place to meet romantic prospects.

Location

The temple was located in south-central Bezantur, the so-called "City of a Thousand Temples". It was quite close to the Temple of Lliira , which was just to the west. It was also bordered by the Temple of Talona and the Temple of Myrkul to the south, the House of Cyric and the Temple of Oghma to the north, and the Temple of Tempus to the east.

Structure

The temple itself was an elegant palace well suited to events that provided opportunities for entertainment and indulgence.

Activities

Owing to the lavish festivals and feasts hosted at the temple, it was a popular place to socialize and meet potential romantic partners, even or especially among those who did not actively worship Sune.

The congregation attracted individuals who were generally not well aligned with the beliefs and attitudes of the ruling Red Wizards of Thay , however they were largely left alone, owing to the fact that they also generally lacked any political power or ambition.

History

Following the opening of the Temple of Sharess in Bezantur, Sune's following began to dwindle as worshipers flocked to the more openly hedonistic delights of Sharess . By the late 1360s DR , Sune's catty worshipers were actively spreading rumors to portray worshipers of Sharess as crude, uncivilized, and debauched.
